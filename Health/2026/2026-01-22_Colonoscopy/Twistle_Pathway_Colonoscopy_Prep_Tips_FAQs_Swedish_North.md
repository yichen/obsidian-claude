# Preparing for Your Colonoscopy
**Helpful Tips and Frequently Asked Questions**

**Providence SWEDISH**

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Please contact your provider or clinic before you come to your procedure if:**
> *   There has been a change in your **MEDICAL CONDITION**.
> *   There have been any changes to the **MEDICATIONS** you are taking.
> *   You have recently been sick or have been in the hospital or to the emergency room.

## Helpful Tips for Your Prep

**Hydration**
Be sure to drink lots of water and other fluids in the days before your procedure. This will help you feel better going through the prep process and the procedure.

**Stay Home**
Plan to stay at home from the time you start the prep process until you leave for your procedure.

### Tips for drinking the liquid prep:
*   **Temperature:** You may drink the prep cold or at room temperature. Many people find it easier to drink the liquid prep when cold. Mix it ahead of time and keep it in the refrigerator. (Do not use ice as this will dilute it and make it less effective).
*   **Chills:** It is normal to feel cold or have the chills when drinking the prep cold.
*   **Dividing Portions:** If using prep in a large container, divide it into smaller containers. This will help make it not so overwhelming to drink it all.
*   **Straw:** Drink through a straw.
*   **Smell:** Put a lid on the cup or hold your nose to avoid the smell.
*   **Taste:** To get the salty taste out of your mouth, try sucking on sweet or sour items like hard candy or lollipops (**no red , purple or blue colored**) (this is ok on clear liquids). Or try menthol cough drops to numb the taste buds before you drink.
*   **Nausea:** If your stomach becomes upset, take a break for 15 to 30 minutes. Then, restart drinking at a slower rate.
*   **Flavoring:** Some liquid preps can be flavored to make them easier to drink. Check with your provider or pharmacists to see if your prescribed prep can be flavored. Flavor your prep with Crystal Light or something similar. A lemon flavor works best.

### Grab a book or your tablet and be prepared for having urgent and watery stools:
*   **Clothing:** Wear loose clothing as you may need to get your pants down quickly.
*   **Timing:** Be prepared, as sometimes the prep starts working right away. However, it may take two to three hours before your first bowel movement. In either case, be ready to spend a lot of time in the bathroom.
*   **Skin Care:** Apply a barrier cream (Vaseline or diaper ointment) to the skin around your anus before bowel movements start. Reapply as needed to prevent irritation. Use wet wipes or hemorrhoid wipes to reduce irritation.
*   **Hydration:** Consider keeping a full water bottle or other clear liquids in the bathroom to stay hydrated, as long as you have finished drinking the prep.
*   **Stool Appearance:** Over time, your bowel movements should be getting clearer and have less solid material. In the last couple hours before your procedure, you should see nothing but clear, yellow fluid with little or no particles.

### When it’s time to leave for your procedure, a few final helpful tips:
*   It may be useful to take an extra pair of underwear and pants for after your procedure.
*   Put a towel or other protection on the seat of the car.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I drive myself home after my procedure?
**NO!** You will be sedated (made sleepy) for your procedure. You will not be allowed to drive or operate heavy machinery until the next day. **You must have a responsible adult, 18 years or older drive or accompany you home after your procedure.** You cannot leave the premises in an Uber/Lyft/Taxi or Bus without an escort (the driver cannot be the escort).

### I accidentally ate seeds or another item on the list that I was not supposed to, can I still have my colonoscopy?
Yes. Usually, only 1 or 2 mistakes will not ruin your preparation or the ability of your provider to perform the colonoscopy.

### Can I continue to take my herbal supplements?
No, please stop taking any herbal or dietary supplements 7 days before your procedure. Many supplements, especially valerian root, garlic, and ginger, can affect blood clotting.

### Can I take my prescription medications while I am doing my colon preparation?
Yes. It is important that you continue to take all your regularly scheduled prescribed medications. You can take your medications up to 4 hours before your procedure unless you are directed otherwise. If you are on medications for diabetes, weight loss, or blood thinners, please follow any specific instructions given to you about these medications by your provider prior to your procedure.

### I take antibiotics before my dental procedures because of a heart murmur, will I need them before my colonoscopy?
That depends on the exact heart problem for which you take antibiotics. Antibiotics are rarely required prior to colonoscopy. Please make sure to mention your heart murmur or problem to your provider.

### I am getting hypoglycemic (low blood sugar), what should I do?
During your prep you can take sugared products, even if you have diabetes. This includes popsicles, Jell-O®, sugared juices, and sugared sodas. Just avoid having anything red, blue, or purple in color as this affects what your provider can see during the procedure.

### When will I begin having bowel movements and what should I do if I don’t have a bowel movement?
Everyone is different. You may start having bowel movements soon after starting the prep or you may not have any until after you have completed the entire prep. It may start suddenly. Once it starts, you will have frequent bowel movements. Each time it should get clearer until you see only clear-ish or yellow-ish fluid with little or no solid matter.

> [!WARNING]
> **YOU MUST FINISH THE ENTIRE PREP directly even if your stool is clear.** Failure to do so may make it difficult for your provider to see everything they need to during your exam, which may result in the need to cancel your colonoscopy and reschedule for another date.

### My skin is raw, or my hemorrhoids are flaring from the frequent diarrhea, what can I do to help?
You can use a barrier cream (Vaseline or diaper ointment) to the skin around your anus that is irritated. You can use any over-the-counter hemorrhoid remedy to ease your discomfort if you have hemorrhoid pain.

### What will I feel like after the procedure?
You should recover from the sedation quickly, but you may feel unsteady at first. **Do not drive, operate machinery, or make any significant decisions until the following day.**

Air is used to inflate your colon during the exam so some minor bloating or abdominal discomfort after the exam is common. You may also continue to have watery bowel movements for a day or more. It may take several days for you to start having normal bowel movements. You may also experience a sore throat or jaw discomfort from being sedated.
