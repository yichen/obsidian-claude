Why? It's because it can do both photo and video. Today, I am carrying a Leica Q2 for taking photos, and a Sony FX3 for shooting videos (to show kids and my family in China). It's a bit cumbersome to carry two cameras in addition to the backpack. If I can manage to make Nikon D850 work, I would just need to carry one camera, without needing to spend a lot of money (maybe just one wider angle lens for video).  Use what equipment you have (Adlerian Psychology)

How hiking makes it possible to synthesis the other ideas?

- Adlerian psychology believe what matters is how one use the given equipment (with self-acceptance) instead of seeking recognition (external motivation). The same idea works with actual physical possessions. I should make use of what I already have, the Nikon D850, instead of seeing to spend more money for a more expensive camera. This week, I've discovered new functions that I don't know my existing camera already have. For example, I forgot that I can use the Leica app to remotely take photos of myself. I noticed that the best way to carry the two cameras (Sony FX3 and Leica Q2), is by carrying Sony FX3 in the small Fjallraven hip bag, and Leica Q2 over the shoulder. This way both cameras are easy to access. I also noticed particular way of configure the Fjallraven hip bag to make the camera easier to access. By focusing on learning what I already have (cameras, the hip bag), I am able to figure out the best way to use them, to the point that I don't need to search for a new product to solve my problem: I've already solved the problem using what I already have. To generalize this idea to me as a person, I should make use of the equipment I have, "this me now", and look for how to make the best use of it. In those cases, I was standing alone (take full responsibility and control), searching for answers inside myself (again, another synthesis of ideas from books I have been reading).
