# 2026-03-01

I camped in my Tesla two weeks ago at Kanaskat-Palmer State Park, just to sleep at the campground and reset my sleep—it worked. This weekend I had no plans or dates and felt a bit lonely and bored at home, so I decided to come back here earlier in the day because the weather was nice. There isn't much going on in the park beyond a short trail by the river, and I also wanted to test my Tesla camping setup.

I brought the external monitor stand for more comfortable laptop use and packed a large external battery because the laptop battery can't power the external LCD monitor for long. The Tesla lap desk is flaky and unstable, so next time I should bring the new car desk I bought in China—it seems stable and heavy, though it might reduce leg space and push the monitor farther away. For short-term working, the external display on the Tesla lap desk is acceptable.

Being in the woods gives me some mental space to reset. I'm conflicted about how to spend alone time on weekends. Camping trips can feel like I'm escaping real life, but intentionally lining up dates feels like dating just for the sake of it—it gets expensive, especially with people who don't have long-term potential. Balancing two jobs, especially with Ben at Slack watching me closely, makes life stressful, so this mental break helps.
