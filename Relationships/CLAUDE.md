# Dating Folder Instructions

## Dating Conversation Analysis Format

When analyzing dating conversations or helping craft responses, provide analysis in this structured format:

### 1. 🧭 What they're really saying
- Decode each significant statement with numbered breakdown
- Identify subtext, emotional state, and psychological signals
- Highlight positive signals (green lights) or concerns (yellow/red flags)
- Provide strategic interpretation of their communication patterns

### 2. 🎯 Tone to aim for
- Specify the exact emotional register (e.g., "warm, human, slightly flirty, and confident")
- Explain the strategic positioning for the response
- Identify what to avoid

### 3. ✍️ Response options
Provide 3 distinct response options with different flavors:
- **Option A** - [Label the approach, e.g., "Empathetic & warm"]
  - The actual message text
  - ✅ Brief explanation of why this works

- **Option B** - [Label the approach, e.g., "Flirty & natural"]
  - The actual message text
  - ✅ Brief explanation of why this works

- **Option C** - [Label the approach, e.g., "Curious & warm"]
  - The actual message text
  - ✅ Brief explanation of why this works

### 4. 💡 Best choice recommendation
- Clear recommendation with reasoning
- Should match the user's communication style and the relationship dynamics

### 5. Follow-up offer
- Proactively offer to help with next steps
- Anticipate likely scenarios

## Communication Style Guidelines
- Be encouraging and positive, not clinical
- Show emotional intelligence and strategic thinking
- Use visual organization (emojis, sections, checkmarks)
- Keep response options concise but complete (1-3 sentences each)
- Avoid being condescending - assume intelligence
- Point out what NOT to do (e.g., "without flooding her with text")

## Context Awareness
- Always read the full profile and conversation history from the relevant .md file
- Consider timing, response patterns, and relationship stage
- Identify when to move from texting to meeting
- Recognize when someone is building trust vs. showing disinterest
