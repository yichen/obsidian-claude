## Why Sedona?

**Unique Features:**
- **Red Rock Formations** - Stunning sandstone formations and canyons
  - [Sedona Red Rock Country](https://www.fs.usda.gov/main/coconino/home) - Coconino National Forest information
- **Vortex Sites** - Energy centers believed to have healing properties
  - [Sedona Vortex Guide](https://www.visitsedona.com/sedona-vortex-guide/) - Official visitor information
- **Iconic Hikes** - Cathedral Rock, Devil's Bridge, Boynton Canyon
- **Dark Sky Designation** - Excellent stargazing
- **Perfect November Weather** - Mild temperatures (60-70°F days), ideal hiking conditions
- **Fewer Crowds** - Less busy than spring/fall peak seasons

**Advantages over other destinations:**
- **World-class hiking** - Iconic red rock trails
- **Unique spiritual energy** - Vortex sites and energy centers
- **Photography paradise** - Dramatic red rock landscapes
- **Walkable town** - Great restaurants and galleries
- **Easy access** - Direct flights to Phoenix, 2-hour scenic drive

## Notes & Reminders

**Trail Etiquette:**
- Stay on designated trails (desert ecosystem is fragile)
- Pack out all trash (Leave No Trace)
- Don't climb on rock formations or arches
- Respect vortex sites and other visitors' meditation
- Be mindful of parking - many trailheads have limited spaces

**Red Rock Pass:**
- Required for parking at most trailheads in Red Rock Country
- Daily pass: $5
- Weekly pass: $15 (better value for 4+ days)
- Annual pass: $20 (if you plan to return)
- Can purchase at visitor center, online, or at some trailheads

**Photography Tips:**
- **Best light:**
  - Sunrise: ~6:45 AM
  - Sunset: ~5:30 PM
  - Golden hour: 4:45-5:45 PM
- **Best photo spots:**
  - Cathedral Rock: Iconic formation, best at sunset
  - Devil's Bridge: Walk out on arch for unique shot
  - Bell Rock: Classic Sedona formation
  - Airport Mesa: Panoramic sunset views
  - Oak Creek Canyon: Beautiful canyon scenery

**Desert Safety:**
- **Bring water** - 1 liter per 2 hours of hiking (more in sun)
- **Sun protection** - Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses (high altitude = strong sun)
- **Layers** - Temperature can change throughout day (cool mornings, warm afternoons)
- **Headlamp** - Shorter days, might finish hikes near dark
- **Emergency gear** - First aid, emergency shelter
- **Trail maps** - Download offline maps, many areas have no cell service

**Altitude Considerations:**
- Sedona is at 4,350 ft elevation
- Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated
- Take breaks if feeling any altitude effects
- Generally mild altitude, but be aware
