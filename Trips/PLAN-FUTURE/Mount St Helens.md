#hiking 

- Most first-time climbers ascend via the Monitor Ridge Route (also known as the Summer Route) from the Climber’s Bivouac Trailhead. Distance 8-10 miles roundtrip. About 4500 feet elevation. The upper section involves significant scrambling over bounders. Most climbers take 7 to 12 hours for the round trip. 
- Prepare: 
    - aim to comfortably hike 10-12 miles with 4000-5000 feet of gain. 
    - Practice hiking on steep trails to prepare for the Boulder fields.
    - Train with a weighted pack (15-20 lbs)
- Essential Gear
    - Hiking boots, layers of clothing (changing weather conditions, including base layer, warm layer, and waterproof/windproof outer shell.
    - Sun protection.
    - Food and water: high-protein snacks and at least 3-4 liters of water.
    - Headlamp
    - Navigation. Phone service is unreliable.
    - First-aid kit
    - Trekking poles
    - Gloves for rough boulders
    - Toiletries: pack out waste
