# Solo Trip Itinerary - Hiking & Sightseeing Focus

## Recommended Hotel: Royal Kona Resort ($1,602 or $1,697)

**Why this is the best basecamp:**
- Central Kona location - best access to hiking/sightseeing across the island
- Oceanfront with sunrise/sunset views
- Walkable to restaurants (no driving after long hiking days)
- Balanced distances: 2h 15m to Volcano, 1h 45m to Mauna Kea, 1h 45m to South Point

**Alternative:** SCP Hilo Hotel ($1,668) - Only if prioritizing east side rainforests/waterfalls, but adds 2+ hours to most west side attractions

---

## Day 1 - Wednesday, October 22: Arrival & Kona Exploration

**Morning/Afternoon:**
- Arrive Kona Airport
- **Drive: 10 minutes (6 miles)** from airport to Royal Kona Resort
- Check in and settle
- Walk around Kailua-Kona historic district (Ali'i Drive right outside hotel)
- Hulihee Palace - **0.5 miles walking** from hotel
- Lunch at local spot (Fish Hopper, Island Lava Java)

**Late Afternoon:**
- **Hike: Kaloko-Honokohau National Historical Park**
- **Drive from hotel: 15 minutes (7 miles)** north
- Easy 1-2 mile coastal trail
- Ancient Hawaiian fishponds, green sea turtle viewing
- Great for jet lag recovery
- https://www.nps.gov/kaho/index.htm

**Evening:**
- Sunset at hotel beach
- Dinner in Kona town (walking distance)

**Total driving: ~30 minutes**

---

## Day 2 - Thursday, October 23: Hawaii Volcanoes National Park (FULL DAY)

**Priority day - Big Island's crown jewel**

**Drive: 2 hours 15 minutes (96 miles)** from Royal Kona Resort via Highway 11 south
- Leave hotel by 7:00 AM, arrive park ~9:15 AM

**Morning:**
- **Kilauea Iki Trail** (4 miles, 3-4 hours, moderate) - **MUST DO**
- Hike across solidified lava lake crater floor
- Steam vents, lush rainforest
- One of the best hikes in Hawaii
- https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/hawaii/kilauea-iki-trail-and-crater-rim-trail

**Midday:**
- Lunch at Volcano House - **5 minutes** from Kilauea Iki trailhead
  - https://www.hawaiivolcanohouse.com/
- Drive **Chain of Craters Road** (40-minute scenic drive / 19 miles to coast)
- Stop at overlooks, walk on old lava flows
- https://www.nps.gov/havo/planyourvisit/chain_of_craters_road.htm

**Afternoon:**
- **Thurston Lava Tube** - **10 minutes** from Volcano House (0.5 mile easy walk)
  - https://www.nps.gov/havo/planyourvisit/nahuku.htm
- **Devastation Trail** - **5 minutes** from Thurston (1 mile boardwalk)
  - https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/hawaii/devastation-trail
- **Steam Vents & Sulphur Banks** - **2 minutes** from park entrance
  - https://www.nps.gov/havo/planyourvisit/hike_sulfur.htm

**Evening:**
- **Drive back to Kona: 2 hours 15 minutes (96 miles)**
- Arrive hotel ~7:30 PM
- Casual dinner near hotel

**Total driving: ~5 hours (round trip + internal park drives)**

**Cost:** $30 park entry pass (valid 7 days)

---

## Day 3 - Friday, October 24: Mauna Kea Summit & Stargazing

**Morning:**
- Sleep in or relax at hotel after yesterday's big day
- Late breakfast in Kona

**Midday:**
- **Drive to Waipio Valley Lookout: 1 hour 45 minutes (65 miles)** north via Highway 19
- Stunning valley overlook (15-minute stop)
- https://www.lovebigisland.com/big-island-valleys/waipio-valley/

**Afternoon:**
- **Continue to Pololu Valley Trailhead: 30 minutes (19 miles)** from Waipio
- **Total from hotel: 2 hours 15 minutes (84 miles)**
- **Hike: Pololu Valley Trail** (2.5 miles RT, 1.5 hours, moderate)
- Black sand beach at bottom, dramatic coastal views
- Northern tip of Big Island
- https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/hawaii/pololu-valley-trail

**Evening (HIGHLIGHT):**
- **Drive to Mauna Kea Visitor Information Station (9,200 ft)**
- **From Pololu: 1 hour 30 minutes (55 miles)** via Highway 19 south, then Saddle Road
- **From hotel (direct): 1 hour 45 minutes (58 miles)**
- **Mauna Kea Summit Sunset & Stargazing**
  - Option 1: Stay at visitor station for stargazing (safe for all vehicles)
  - Option 2: If 4WD rental, drive to summit (13,796 ft) - **30 minutes** from visitor station on unpaved road
- World's best stargazing - crystal clear skies
- Bring warm layers (30-40°F at night)
- https://www.ifa.hawaii.edu/info/vis/
- **Return drive to hotel: 1 hour 45 minutes (58 miles)**

**Total driving: ~7 hours (big driving day, but worth it)**

**Note:** Spend 30 min at visitor station before going higher (acclimatization)

---

## Day 4 - Saturday, October 25: South Kona Coast & Snorkeling

**Morning:**
- **Drive to Captain Cook Monument Trailhead: 30 minutes (14 miles)** south via Highway 11
- **Hike: Captain Cook Monument Trail** (5 miles RT, 3 hours, moderate-difficult)
- Steep descent to Kealakekua Bay
- **Best snorkeling on Big Island** (bring gear!)
- Challenging climb back up (1,300 ft elevation gain)
- **Start early (7:00 AM)** - gets hot on climb back
- https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/hawaii/kealakekua-bay-state-historical-park

**Afternoon:**
- **Drive to Pu'uhonua o Honaunau National Historical Park: 15 minutes (7 miles)** south
- **Total from hotel: 45 minutes (21 miles)**
- Ancient Hawaiian sanctuary, great tide pools, historic temples
- Easy walking
- https://www.nps.gov/puho/index.htm

**Late Afternoon:**
- **Drive to South Point (Ka Lae): 1 hour (32 miles)** south from Pu'uhonua
- **Total from hotel: 1 hour 45 minutes (53 miles)**
- Southernmost point in USA
- Green Sand Beach (Papakolea) nearby - **2.5 miles** hike or 4WD from South Point
- Dramatic cliffs and wind
- https://www.lovebigisland.com/big-island-activities/south-point/
- Green Sand Beach: https://www.lovebigisland.com/big-island-beaches/green-sand-beach/

**Evening:**
- **Return drive to Kona: 1 hour 45 minutes (53 miles)** north via Highway 11
- Arrive hotel ~6:00 PM
- Dinner in Kona town

**Total driving: ~5 hours**

## Logistics & Tips

### Rental Car:
- **Get 4WD if possible** - opens up Mauna Kea summit, Green Sand Beach access
- Many agencies restrict 4WD to summit roads (check Costco package policy)
- **Gas up in Kona before long drives** - limited stations on Highway 19 north and Saddle Road
- **Fill tank before Volcano day** - gas is expensive inside the park
- **Estimated gas cost:** ~$100-120 (665 miles ÷ 25 mpg × $4/gallon)

### Weather (Late October):
- **Kona side (hotel):** 75-85°F, mostly sunny (dry season)
- **Volcano area:** 60-70°F, possible rain
- **Mauna Kea summit:** 30-45°F, very cold at night
- **North Shore:** 75-80°F, occasional showers

### Reservations Needed:
- None! All hikes and sites are first-come, first-served
- Just show up early for popular trails (Kilauea Iki by 8:00 AM)

### Food Strategy:
- **Breakfast:** Hotel or grab-and-go from Kona coffee shops
- **Lunch:** Pack sandwiches for hiking days (Safeway in Kona - **5 minutes** from hotel)
- **Dinner:** Kona restaurants (Da Poke Shack, Umekes, Humpy's Big Island Alehouse)
- **Estimated food cost:** ~$200 (4 dinners + groceries for lunches)

### Safety:
- Tell someone your hiking plans each day
- Carry extra water (volcanic terrain = exposed, hot)
- Check volcano.gov for eruption updates
- Cell service is spotty in remote areas (Mauna Kea, South Point)
