## Accommodation Options

**Why stay in Bend:**
- Central location for all activities (Smith Rock 30min, Newberry 45min, Cascade Lakes 1hr)
- Excellent restaurant scene walkable from downtown (important after long hikes)
- WiFi for Thursday work session
- No setup/teardown time in cold mornings
- Warm showers after hiking

**Hotel Recommendations:**

1. **Oxford Hotel Bend** (Downtown) - TOP CHOICE

2. **McMenamins Old St. Francis School** (Downtown)
   - 700 NW Bond St, Bend, OR 97703
   - Unique historic property
   - On-site restaurant and soaking pool (great after hiking!)
   - Rating: 4.3/5

3. **Wall Street Suites** (Downtown)
   - 1430 NW Wall St, Bend, OR 97703
   - Budget-friendly
   - Still walkable to restaurants

### Option 2: Camping Near Bend (Budget Option)

**Camping advantages:**
- **Save $220-345** vs hotels
- Closer to nature, campfire experience
- Can cook own meals (additional savings)
- More adventurous

**Camping disadvantages:**
- Cold October nights (28-38°F lows) - need warm sleeping gear
- No walkable restaurants after long hikes (must drive or cook)
- Daily drive to/from campground adds time/gas
- No WiFi for Thursday work (need cafe anyway)
- Setup/teardown in freezing mornings
- More gear to pack

**Recommended Campgrounds:**

1. **Tumalo State Park** - BEST OPTION
   - Address: 62976 O B Riley Rd, Bend, OR 97701
   - **Distance from Bend:** 5 miles NW (10 min drive)
   - **Cost:** $24-34/night (full hookup sites), $19-22/night (tent sites)
   - **Total 4 nights:** ~**$76-136**
   - **Facilities:** Showers, flush toilets, water, firewood for sale
   - **Reservations:** [Reserve online](https://oregonstateparks.reserveamerica.com) - book NOW for October
   - **Why it's good:** Closest to Bend, good facilities, along Deschutes River
   - **Phone:** (541) 388-6055

2. **La Pine State Park**
   - Address: 15800 State Recreation Rd, La Pine, OR 97739
   - **Distance from Bend:** 30 miles south (35 min drive)
   - **Cost:** $22-32/night
   - **Total 4 nights:** ~**$88-128**
   - **Facilities:** Showers, flush toilets, firewood, camp store
   - **Why it's good:** Large campground, better availability, near Newberry
   - **Downside:** 35+ min drive to/from Bend each day (adds 70 min daily driving + gas)
   - **Phone:** (541) 536-2428

3. **Deschutes National Forest - Dispersed Camping** (FREE)
   - Various locations along Forest Service roads
   - **Cost:** FREE
   - **Facilities:** NONE (primitive camping - bring everything)
   - **No reservations** - first-come, first-served
   - **Why it's good:** Free, solitude, flexibility
   - **Downside:** No water, toilets, or showers; remote; need to research spots
   - **Best areas:** Off Cascade Lakes Scenic Byway, near Sparks Lake
   - **Info:** Call Bend-Fort Rock Ranger District (541) 383-4000

**Camping Cost Estimate:**
- Campground fees: $76-136 (4 nights)
- Extra gas (daily campground driving): $20-30
- Ice for cooler: $10-15
- Firewood: $20-30 (optional)
- Groceries for camp cooking: $50-100 (if cooking vs eating out)
- **Total camping trip:** $640-835 (vs $950-1,050 hotel trip)
- **Savings:** $220-345

**If camping, adjust itinerary:**
- Wednesday: Arrive Bend, grocery shop, drive to campground, setup, THEN Smith Rock (tighter timeline)
- Thursday morning: Work from cafe (Thump Coffee) instead of hotel/campground
- Evenings: Cook at camp OR drive to Bend for restaurants (adds 20-60 min round trip)
- Need to account for campground check-out time on Sunday

- **Elevation gain:** 1,500 feet

- **Difficulty:** Moderate-Challenging

**Highlights:**
- **Summit elevation: 7,985 feet** - highest point in monument
- 360° views of entire Newberry Caldera
- See both Paulina Lake and East Lake in the crater below
- Views of Mt. Bachelor, Three Sisters, Mt. Hood, Mt. Jefferson
- Clear day visibility: 50+ miles
- One of Oregon's best panoramic viewpoints

**Alternative shorter hikes at Newberry:**

## Hotel vs Camping: Quick Decision Guide

**Choose HOTELS if:**
- You want convenience and comfort after long hikes
- You need reliable WiFi for work (Thursday)
- You prefer walkable restaurants/nightlife
- You want warm showers and cozy beds
- Cold weather camping (28-38°F nights) doesn't appeal
- Budget allows $950-1,050 total trip

**Choose CAMPING if:**
- You want to save $220-345
- You enjoy campfire cooking and outdoor experience
- You have warm sleeping gear (0-20°F bag)
- You're okay with longer daily driving to/from campground
- You can work from cafes (no WiFi at camp)
- You want more adventurous/rustic experience
- Budget is $640-835 total trip

**My recommendation:** Hotels, specifically Oxford Hotel or McMenamins
- Walkable restaurants eliminate driving after exhausting hikes
- Better for solo trip (social/dining options)
- October weather makes camping less appealing (freezing mornings)
- Work-friendly setup for Thursday
- Convenience worth the extra $220-345 for 4-day trip
