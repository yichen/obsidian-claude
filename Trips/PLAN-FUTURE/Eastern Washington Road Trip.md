## Why Eastern Washington?

**Unique Features:**
- **Channeled Scablands** - carved by massive Ice Age floods
  - [Channeled Scablands Photos](https://www.alisonmeyerphotography.com/gallery/palouse) - Rolling hills and geological features
  - [Scablands Geology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Channeled_Scablands) - Information about Ice Age flood features
- **Palouse Hills** - rolling wheat fields and buttes
  - [Palouse Hills Wide View](https://www.craiggoodwinphoto.com/photo/palouse-hills-wide-view) - Panoramic view from Steptoe Butte
  - [Green Rolling Hills](https://vastphotos.com/photo/the-green-rolling-hills-of-the-palouse-by-greg-probst) - High-resolution photo of the Palouse
- **Dry Falls** - ancient waterfall 5x larger than Niagara
  - [Dry Falls State Park](https://parks.wa.gov/533/Dry-Falls) - Official park information
  - [Dry Falls Geology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dry_Falls) - Geological significance and photos
- **Steptoe Butte** - panoramic views of the Palouse
  - [Steptoe Butte Views](https://www.duncan.co/rolling-hills-of-the-palouse-from-steptoe-butte) - Rolling hills from the butte
  - [Steptoe Butte State Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steptoe_Butte) - Park information and history
- **Grand Coulee Dam** - engineering marvel
  - [Grand Coulee Dam Visitor Center](https://www.usbr.gov/pn/grandcoulee/) - Official visitor information
  - [Grand Coulee Dam Photos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Coulee_Dam) - Engineering marvel images
- **Drier weather** than Central Oregon in October

**Advantages over Central Oregon:**
- **Better weather** - less rain, more sunshine
- **Shorter drive** - 3.5 hours vs 4.5 hours to Bend
- **Unique geology** - glacial flood features you can't see elsewhere
- **Less crowded** - fewer tourists than popular Oregon destinations
- **Lower costs** - more affordable accommodations

- **Charging Time:** ~8-12 hours for full charge (overnight charging)

- **Charging Plan:**
  1. **Start with full charge** in Sammamish
  2. **Overnight charging** at campgrounds (primary method)

## Notes & Reminders

**Trail Etiquette:**
- Yield to uphill hikers
- Stay on designated trails
- Pack out all trash (Leave No Trace)
- Dogs allowed on most trails (leash required)

**Photography Tips:**
- **Best light:**
  - Sunrise: 7:20 AM
  - Sunset: 6:15 PM
  - Golden hour: 5:30-6:30 PM
- **Best photo spots:**
  - Steptoe Butte: 360° Palouse views

  - Dry Falls: Ancient flood features
  - Grand Coulee Dam: Engineering marvel

**High Desert Tips:**
- **Sun is intense** - even at cooler temps, wear sunscreen
- **Low humidity** - drink extra water
- **Temperature swings** - 35°F mornings, 65°F afternoons (bring layers!)
- **Wind** - Steptoe Butte can be very windy
