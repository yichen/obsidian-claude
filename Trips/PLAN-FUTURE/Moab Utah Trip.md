## Why Moab?

**Unique Features:**
- **Arches National Park** - over 2,000 natural stone arches
  - [Arches National Park](https://www.nps.gov/arch/index.htm) - Official park information
  - [Delicate Arch](https://www.nps.gov/arch/planyourvisit/delicate-arch.htm) - Iconic arch hiking information
- **Canyonlands National Park** - vast canyons and mesas
  - [Canyonlands National Park](https://www.nps.gov/cany/index.htm) - Official park information
  - [Mesa Arch](https://www.nps.gov/thingstodo/mesa-arch.htm) - Famous sunrise arch
- **Dead Horse Point State Park** - panoramic Colorado River views
  - [Dead Horse Point](https://stateparks.utah.gov/parks/dead-horse-point/) - State park information
  - [Park Overview & Viewpoints](https://stateparks.utah.gov/parks/dead-horse-point/) - Official info and viewpoints
  - [AllTrails - Dead Horse Point State Park](https://www.alltrails.com/parks/us/utah/dead-horse-point-state-park) - Trails and maps
- **Desert landscapes** - red rock formations and unique geology
- **Perfect weather** - mild temperatures, ideal for hiking
- **Fewer crowds** than summer season

**Advantages over other destinations:**
- **World-class hiking** - iconic trails and formations
- **Unique geology** - arches and canyons found nowhere else
- **Perfect weather** - 60-70°F days, cool nights
- **Photography paradise** - dramatic red rock landscapes
- **Easy access** - direct flights available

## Flight Options

### **Option 1: Direct to Moab (Recommended)**
- **Route:** Seattle (SEA) → Denver (DEN) → Moab (CNY)
- **Airlines:** Alaska Airlines + Contour Airlines
- **Total time:** ~4 hours (including layover)
- **Cost:** ~$400-600 round trip
- **Advantages:** Direct to Moab, no long drive
- **Disadvantages:** Limited flight times, more expensive

### **Option 2: Salt Lake City + Drive**
- **Route:** Seattle (SEA) → Salt Lake City (SLC) → Drive to Moab
- **Airlines:** Alaska Airlines, Delta
- **Drive time:** 3.5 hours (222 miles)
- **Cost:** ~$300-400 round trip + rental car
- **Advantages:** More flight options, cheaper
- **Disadvantages:** Long drive, but scenic

### **Option 3: Grand Junction + Drive**
- **Route:** Seattle (SEA) → Grand Junction (GJT) → Drive to Moab
- **Airlines:** Alaska Airlines, United
- **Drive time:** 1.5 hours (97 miles)
- **Cost:** ~$350-450 round trip + rental car
- **Advantages:** Short drive, scenic route
- **Disadvantages:** Limited flight options

**Recommended:** Option 1 (Direct to Moab) for convenience, or Option 2 (SLC) for cost savings

### Day 2 - Thursday, October 23: Arches National Park

**WORK BUFFER - Morning**
- **7:30 AM** - Breakfast at hotel
- **8:00 AM - 11:00 AM** - Work session from hotel
- **WiFi:** Available at hotel

**11:00 AM** - Drive to Arches National Park
- **Drive from Moab: 10 minutes (5 miles)**
- **Park entrance fee:** $30 (or use America the Beautiful Pass)
- **Visitor Center:** Maps and information

**12:00 PM - 6:00 PM: Arches Hiking**
- **Delicate Arch Trail** - 3.2 miles round trip, moderate, 2-3 hours
  - **Highlights:**
    - Iconic arch (most photographed in Utah)
    - Red rock formations
    - **Perfect for photography** - best light in afternoon
    - **Safety:** Bring water, sun protection
  - AllTrails: https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/delicate-arch-trail
  - YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Delicate+Arch+hike
- **Windows Section** - 1.2 miles round trip, easy, 1 hour
  - **Highlights:** North Window, South Window, Turret Arch
  - AllTrails (Windows Loop & Turret Arch): https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/the-windows-loop-trail-and-turret-arch
  - YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Windows+Section+Arches+National+Park
- **Double Arch** - 0.5 miles round trip, easy, 30 minutes
  - **Highlights:** Two arches sharing the same rock fin
  - AllTrails: https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/double-arch-trail
  - YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Double+Arch+trail

**6:30 PM** - Return to Moab
- **Dinner:** Desert Bistro (fine dining)
- **Evening:** Relax at hotel

**Total Day 2 Driving: ~1 hour**

---

### Day 3 - Friday, October 24: Canyonlands National Park

**8:00 AM** - Breakfast and prepare for long day
- Pack lunch, water, layers (canyon can be windy)

**9:00 AM - Drive to Canyonlands National Park**
- **Drive from Moab: 45 minutes (35 miles)** via US-191 S
- **Island in the Sky District** - most accessible area
- **Visitor Center:** Maps and trail information

**10:00 AM - 5:00 PM: Canyonlands Hiking**
- **Mesa Arch Trail** - 0.5 miles round trip, easy, 30 minutes
  - **Highlights:**
    - **Famous sunrise arch** (best at sunrise, but beautiful anytime)
    - Canyon views through the arch
    - **Perfect for photography** - iconic shot
  - AllTrails: https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/mesa-arch-trail
  - YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Mesa+Arch+sunrise
- **Grand View Point Trail** - 2 miles round trip, easy, 1.5 hours
  - **Highlights:**
    - **360° panoramic views** of canyons
    - Colorado River views
    - **Best viewpoint** in the park
  - AllTrails: https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/grand-view-point-trail
  - YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Grand+View+Point+Trail
- **Upheaval Dome** - 1.8 miles round trip, moderate, 1.5 hours
  - **Highlights:**
    - **Unique geological formation** - meteorite impact crater
    - Canyon views
    - **Geological mystery** - still debated origin
  - AllTrails (Overlook): https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/utah/upheaval-dome-overlook-trail
  - YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Upheaval+Dome+overlook+trail

**5:30 PM** - Return to Moab
- **Drive: 45 minutes (35 miles)**

**Evening:**
- **Dinner:** Moab Brewery (local beer and food)
- **Relax** after long hiking day

**Total Day 3 Driving: ~2 hours**

## Notes & Reminders

**Trail Etiquette:**
- Stay on designated trails
- Pack out all trash (Leave No Trace)
- Don't climb on arches or formations
- Respect wildlife

**Photography Tips:**
- **Best light:**
  - Sunrise: 7:20 AM
  - Sunset: 6:15 PM
  - Golden hour: 6:00-7:00 PM
- **Best photo spots:**
  - Delicate Arch: Iconic arch
  - Mesa Arch: Sunrise through arch
  - Dead Horse Point: Canyon views
  - Windows Section: Multiple arches

**Desert Safety:**
- **Bring water** - 1 gallon per person per day
- **Sun protection** - Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses
- **Layers** - Temperature changes dramatically
- **Emergency gear** - First aid, emergency shelter
