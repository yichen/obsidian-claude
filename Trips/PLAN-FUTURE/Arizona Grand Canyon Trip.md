# Arizona: Sedona & Grand Canyon Trip Plan
**Dates:** October 22-26, 2025 (Wednesday - Sunday)
**Trip Type:** Solo hiking and sightseeing
**Focus:** Red rock hiking, vortex energy, and epic canyon views.

---

## High-Level Plan
- **Leave:** Wednesday 10/22 morning
- **Return:** Sunday 10/26 evening
- **Total Days:** 4 full days + travel
- **Focus:** A progressive road trip exploring the spiritual red rocks of Sedona and the immense scale of the Grand Canyon.
- **Route:** Fly into Phoenix (PHX) → Sedona (2 nights) → Grand Canyon South Rim (2 nights) → Fly out of Phoenix (PHX).

---

## Why Arizona in October?

**Unique Features:**
- **Sedona's Red Rocks & Vortexes:** Hike among stunning sandstone formations, with options for unique spiritual and energy-focused experiences.
- **Grand Canyon South Rim:** Witness one of the world's most awe-inspiring natural wonders. Hike below the rim for a unique perspective.
- **Perfect Hiking Weather:** October offers ideal conditions—sunny, 65-75°F days and cool, crisp nights. It's peak season for a reason.
- **Stargazing:** Both Sedona and the Grand Canyon are designated Dark Sky Communities/Parks, offering spectacular night sky views.

**Advantages over other options:**
- **Excellent Alternative to Utah:** Offers a similar "red rock" vibe but with the distinct character of Sedona's forests and the singular majesty of the Grand Canyon.
- **Efficient Loop:** The Phoenix → Sedona → Grand Canyon → Phoenix route is a very logical and scenic loop with manageable daily driving.
- **Variety:** Combines the vibrant, almost mystical energy of Sedona with the raw, immense, and humbling nature of the Grand Canyon.

## Detailed Itinerary

### Day 1 - Wednesday, October 22: Arrival & Sedona Sunset
**Theme:** Travel and first taste of the red rocks.

**Morning/Afternoon:**
- **~9:00 AM:** Depart Seattle (SEA) on a morning flight to Phoenix (PHX).
- **~12:00 PM:** Arrive in Phoenix. Pick up rental car.
- Grab a quick lunch and supplies (water, snacks) near the airport.

**Afternoon:**
- **Drive to Sedona:** 2 hours (120 miles) north via I-17 N.
- **3:30 PM:** Arrive in Sedona and check into your hotel.

**Late Afternoon - Sunset Hike:**
- **Hike: Cathedral Rock Trail**
  - **Drive from hotel:** 10-15 minutes.
  - **Details:** 1.2 miles round-trip, but it's a steep, challenging scramble requiring hands and feet. The payoff is phenomenal.
  - **Duration:** 1.5 - 2 hours.
  - **Goal:** Reach the saddle between the spires for sunset. The view and energy are legendary.
  - **AllTrails:** [Cathedral Rock Trail](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/arizona/cathedral-rock-trail)
  - **Note:** Bring a headlamp for the hike down after sunset.

**Evening:**
- **Dinner:** Walk to a restaurant in Uptown Sedona or near your hotel.
- **Stargazing:** Step outside and look up. Sedona's dark skies are impressive.

**Total Day 1 Driving:** ~2.5 hours
**Hotel:** Sedona, AZ (2 nights)

---

### Day 2 - Thursday, October 23: Sedona's Best Hikes
**Theme:** Deep dive into Sedona's iconic trails.

**WORK BUFFER - Morning (Optional):**
- **7:30 AM - 10:30 AM:** Enjoy breakfast, then use the hotel WiFi for your work session.

**Late Morning/Afternoon:**
- **Hike: Devil's Bridge Trail**
  - **Drive from hotel:** 20 minutes.
  - **Details:** 4.2 miles round-trip, moderate. The final stretch is a steep rock staircase.
  - **Duration:** 2-3 hours.
  - **Highlight:** Walk out onto the largest natural sandstone arch in the area for an iconic photo. Go early to avoid long lines for the photo op.
  - **AllTrails:** [Devil's Bridge Trail](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/arizona/devils-bridge-trail)

**Afternoon:**
- **Hike: Boynton Canyon Trail**
  - **Drive from Devil's Bridge:** 15 minutes.
  - **Details:** 6.1 miles round-trip, easy/moderate. A beautiful canyon walk.
  - **Duration:** 3-4 hours.
  - **Highlight:** Known for its powerful vortex energy. A side trail leads to the "Kachina Woman" and "Warrior Rock" vortex sites.
  - **AllTrails:** [Boynton Canyon Trail](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/arizona/boynton-canyon-trail)

**Evening:**
- **Dinner:** Explore another of Sedona's excellent restaurants.
- Relax and prepare for the drive to the Grand Canyon.

**Total Day 2 Driving:** ~1 hour

---

### Day 3 - Friday, October 24: Drive to the Grand Canyon & Rim Views
**Theme:** Scenic drive and first look at the chasm.

**Morning:**
- **8:00 AM:** Breakfast in Sedona.
- **9:00 AM:** Begin the scenic drive to the Grand Canyon South Rim.
- **Route:** Take AZ-89A North through Oak Creek Canyon, one of the most scenic drives in Arizona. Then I-40 W to AZ-64 N.
- **Total Drive:** 2 hours 15 minutes (115 miles).

**Late Morning:**
- **11:30 AM:** Arrive at Grand Canyon National Park.
- Check into your lodge or hotel in Tusayan (just outside the park).
- **Lunch:** Grab a bite at the Maswik Lodge food court or a cafe in the Grand Canyon Village.

**Afternoon:**
- **Explore the Rim Trail:**
  - Walk east from the main Visitor Center towards Mather Point, Yavapai Point, and the Yavapai Geology Museum.
  - This is a paved, easy walk with non-stop, jaw-dropping views.
  - **Distance:** Walk as little or as much as you like (e.g., 2-3 miles).
  - **Duration:** 2-3 hours, including stops for photos and contemplation.

**Late Afternoon - Sunset:**
- **Drive Hermit Road:** This scenic road is open to private vehicles after December 1st, but in October you'll use the free park shuttle bus.
- **Action:** Hop on the shuttle bus (Red Route) and ride it to **Hopi Point** or **Mohave Point**.
- **Highlight:** These are two of the best spots on the South Rim to watch the sunset. The changing colors on the canyon walls are unforgettable.

**Evening:**
- **Dinner:** El Tovar Dining Room (make reservations far in advance for a fine dining experience) or the more casual Arizona Steakhouse.
- **Stargazing:** The Grand Canyon is an International Dark Sky Park. Find a dark spot away from the village lights (Mather Point is great) and enjoy the Milky Way.

**Total Day 3 Driving:** ~2.5 hours
**Hotel:** Grand Canyon Village or Tusayan, AZ (2 nights)

---

### Day 4 - Saturday, October 25: Hiking Below the Rim
**Theme:** Gaining a new perspective of the canyon.

**Morning (Start Early!):**
- **7:30 AM:** Grab a quick breakfast and drive to the trailhead.
- **Hike: South Kaibab Trail to Ooh Aah Point and Cedar Ridge**
  - **Trailhead:** Access via the park shuttle (Orange Route).
  - **Destination 1: Ooh Aah Point.** 1.8 miles round-trip, 600 ft elevation change. The name says it all. A perfect short objective. (1-1.5 hours)
  - **Destination 2: Cedar Ridge.** 3 miles round-trip, 1,140 ft elevation change. A great spot for a snack break with panoramic views and restrooms. (2-3 hours)
  - **CRITICAL RULE:** Hiking down is optional, hiking up is mandatory. It takes twice as long to hike up as it does to go down. Do not underestimate the canyon.
  - **AllTrails:** [South Kaibab Trail to Ooh Aah Point](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/arizona/south-kaibab-trail-to-ooh-aah-point)

**Afternoon:**
- After your hike, have a well-deserved lunch.
- **Drive Desert View Drive:**
  - Take your car east along the rim for 25 miles.
  - Stop at viewpoints like **Grandview Point**, **Moran Point**, and **Lipan Point**.
  - **Final Stop: Desert View Watchtower.** Climb the historic watchtower for a unique view of the canyon and the Colorado River bending south.

**Evening:**
- Enjoy a final canyon sunset from Yavapai Point or Mather Point.
- **Final Dinner:** Choose a restaurant in Tusayan or the Grand Canyon Village.
- Pack for your departure.

**Total Day 4 Driving:** ~2 hours (scenic drive)

---

### Day 5 - Sunday, October 26: Return to Phoenix
**Theme:** One last look and travel home.

**Morning:**
- **Sunrise:** Catch one last sunrise over the canyon from Mather Point.
- **8:00 AM:** Breakfast.
- **9:00 AM:** Check out and begin the drive back to Phoenix.
- **Drive to Phoenix (PHX):** 3.5 hours (230 miles) south via AZ-64 S and I-17 S.

**Afternoon:**
- **12:30 PM:** Arrive in the Phoenix area. Grab lunch.
- **2:30 PM:** Return rental car at PHX.
- **~4:30 PM:** Evening flight from Phoenix (PHX) to Seattle (SEA).

**Total Day 5 Driving:** ~3.5 hours

## Accommodation Recommendations

### Sedona (2 nights):
- **Mid-Range:** **Sky Rock Inn of Sedona.** Great views, well-located.
- **Value:** **The Wilde Resort and Spa.** Good amenities, comfortable.
- **Location is key:** Stay in Uptown or West Sedona for walkability to restaurants.

### Grand Canyon (2 nights):
- **In-Park (Best):** **Thunderbird Lodge** or **Kachina Lodge.** Located directly on the rim. Book 6-12 months in advance.
- **In-Park (Value):** **Maswik Lodge** or **Yavapai Lodge.** Set back from the rim but still in the park.
- **Outside Park (Tusayan):** **Best Western Premier Grand Canyon Squire Inn.** 10-minute drive to the rim, more dining options.

## Logistics & Tips

*   **Reservations:** Book your Grand Canyon lodging AS SOON AS POSSIBLE. It fills up nearly a year in advance. Sedona hotels are easier but book 1-2 months out for good rates.
*   **Altitude:** Sedona is at 4,350 ft; the Grand Canyon South Rim is at 7,000 ft. Drink plenty of water to acclimate and stay hydrated, especially when hiking.
*   **What to Pack:**
    *   Layers are essential. Mornings will be cold (30-40°F at the Grand Canyon), while afternoons will be pleasant (65-75°F).
    *   Broken-in hiking boots, hiking poles (especially for the canyon).
    *   Daypack, sunscreen, sun hat, and a headlamp.
*   **Work Session:** Thursday morning is the ideal time for your work block, with good WiFi in Sedona before you head to the more remote Grand Canyon.
