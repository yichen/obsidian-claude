## Recommended Trails to Try Next

**All trails below verified as OPEN as of November 2, 2025**

### High Priority - Unique Experiences:

#### 1. **Alaka'i Swamp Trail to Kilohana Lookout**
- **Difficulty:** Challenging
- **Distance:** 7.0 miles out-and-back
- **Time:** ~3-4 hours
- **Location:** Koke'e State Park area
- **Highlights:** Unique swamp terrain (one of world's highest rainforests), boardwalk sections, panoramic views of Hanalei Bay
- **AllTrails:** https://www.alltrails.com/trail/hawaii/kaua-i--2/alaka-i-swamp-trail
- **Notes:** Can be very muddy, boardwalk sections can be slippery. Bring proper footwear.
- **Why it's different:** Unique swamp ecosystem you haven't experienced yet
- **Status:** ✅ Verified OPEN

#### 2. **Uluwehi Secret Falls via Wailua River**
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Distance:** 6.1 miles (includes kayaking + hiking)
- **Time:** ~3 hours total
- **Location:** Wailua River area
- **Highlights:** Combines kayaking and hiking, beautiful Secret Falls
- **AllTrails:** https://www.alltrails.com/trail/hawaii/kaua-i--2/uluwehi-secret-falls-via-wailua-river
- **Notes:** Requires kayaking portion (can rent kayaks). Different experience from pure hiking.
- **Why it's different:** Water-based approach + waterfall - unique combo
- **Status:** ✅ Verified OPEN

#### 3. **Okolehao Trail**
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Distance:** 2.5 miles round trip
- **Time:** ~1.5-2 hours
- **Location:** Near Hanalei, north shore
- **Highlights:** Panoramic views of Hanalei Bay and surrounding taro fields
- **AllTrails:** https://www.alltrails.com/trail/hawaii/kaua-i--2/okolehao-viewpoint-via-okolehao-trail
- **Notes:** Can be muddy, some steep sections. Great for shorter day.
- **Why it's different:** Different views of north shore you haven't seen
- **Status:** ✅ Verified OPEN

---

### Moderate Priority - Scenic & Accessible:

#### 4. **Iliau Nature Loop + Kukui Trail (Waimea Canyon area)**
- **Difficulty:** Easy to Moderate
- **Distance:** Iliau Loop is 0.3 miles (easy); Kukui Trail is 5 miles one-way (challenging, descends into canyon)
- **Location:** Waimea Canyon / Koke'e State Park
- **Highlights:** Canyon views, native plants
- **AllTrails:** https://www.alltrails.com/trail/hawaii/kaua-i--2/iliau-nature-loop-trail (Iliau) or https://www.alltrails.com/trail/hawaii/kaua-i--2/kukui-trail (Kukui)
- **Notes:** Iliau is easy warm-up; Kukui descends 2000+ feet into canyon (steep return)
- **Why it's different:** Different perspective of Waimea Canyon
- **Status:** ✅ Verified OPEN

#### 5. **Makaleha Falls Trail**
- **Difficulty:** Challenging
- **Distance:** 2.6 miles out-and-back
- **Time:** ~2.5 hours
- **Location:** Lihue area
- **Highlights:** Multiple stream crossings, lush jungle, beautiful waterfall
- **AllTrails:** https://www.alltrails.com/trail/hawaii/kaua-i--2/makaleha-trail
- **Notes:** Very muddy, multiple stream crossings (water shoes helpful)
- **Why it's different:** Dense jungle waterfall experience
- **Status:** ✅ Verified OPEN

## Important Notes

1. **Weather-dependent closures** are very common - Kauai trails close frequently after rain (check status before each hike)
2. **Permits may be required** - Check DLNR website for any permit requirements
3. **Cell service is limited** - Many trailheads have no cell service (as you noted in October trip)
4. **Mud conditions** - Most trails get very muddy, especially Alaka'i Swamp Trail
5. **Stream crossings** - Several trails have stream crossings (water shoes or sandals helpful)
6. **Trail status can change** - While verified open as of November 2, 2025, always check AllTrails recent reviews before hiking
