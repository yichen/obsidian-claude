## Day 2 - Thursday, November 6: Best Snorkeling Day - Kealakekua Bay

**WORK SCHEDULE:** Work until 10:00 AM at hotel

**Late Morning/Afternoon:**
- **10:30 AM** - Drive to **Kealakekua Bay Snorkel Tour departure** (~30 min)
- **11:00 AM - 3:00 PM** - **Captain Cook Monument Snorkel Tour** ⭐️ MUST DO
  - **Best snorkeling in all of Hawaii** (world-renowned!)
  - Crystal clear waters, spinner dolphins, tropical fish, coral reefs
  - Boat tour includes snorkel gear, lunch, drinks
  - Only accessible by boat or kayak
  - Book in advance: Fair Wind Cruises, Sea Quest, or Captain Cook Snorkeling Cruises
  - Cost: ~$150-180/person
  - https://www.fair-wind.com/morning-kealakekua-snorkel-tour/
  - https://www.lovebigisland.com/kealakekua-bay/

**Afternoon:**
- **3:30 PM** - Return to harbor
- **4:00 PM** - Drive to **Pu'uhonua o Honaunau National Historical Park** (15 min south)
  - "Place of Refuge" - ancient Hawaiian sanctuary
  - Self-guided walk (~30-45 min) on crushed coral/lava rock
  - Great Wall (12 ft high!), temples, tiki statues, tide pools
  - Easy recovery after snorkeling
  - Entry: $20/vehicle (or use National Parks Pass)
  - https://www.nps.gov/puho/index.htm

**Evening:**
- **6:00 PM** - Return to Kona (45 min drive)
- **7:00 PM** - Dinner in Kona

**Total driving: ~2 hours**

**Cost:** $150-180 (snorkel tour) + $20 (park entry)

## Day 4 - Saturday, November 8: Manta Ray Night Snorkel & Coastal Hike

**Full Day Available!**

  - https://www.lovebigisland.com/kiholo-bay/

**Afternoon:**

  - https://mantaadventures.com/

## Logistics & Tips

### Rental Car:
- **Standard 2WD car is fine!** - No need for 4WD
- **Gas cost:** ~$40-50 (200 miles ÷ 25 mpg × $4/gallon) - **saves $60-70!**
- Gas stations plentiful in Kona area
- Free parking at most beaches and parks

### Weather (November - Kona Side):
- **Kona coast:** 75-82°F, mostly sunny (**driest area on Big Island!**)
- **Ocean temp:** 77-79°F year-round (perfect for snorkeling!)
- **Note:** Kona is in rain shadow - way less rain than Hilo side
- **November is excellent** for Kona - dry season, calm waters

### Reservations HIGHLY RECOMMENDED:
- **Kealakekua Bay Snorkel Tour** (Day 2) - Book 1-2 weeks ahead
  - Fair Wind Cruises, Sea Quest, Captain Cook Snorkeling
- **Manta Ray Night Snorkel** (Day 4) - Book 1-2 weeks ahead
  - Manta Adventures, Sunlight on Water, Sea Quest
- **Kona Coffee Farm Tours** - Most are walk-in friendly, but check hours

### Food Strategy:
- **Breakfast:** Hotel or Kona coffee shops (Island Lava Java, Daylight Mind)
- **Lunch:** Pack for boat tours, restaurants in Kona
- **Dinner:**
  - Huggo's on the Rocks (oceanfront casual)
  - Da Poke Shack (local favorite poke bowls)
  - Umekes Fish Market (fresh fish)
  - Humpy's Big Island Alehouse (craft beer + pub food)
  - Jackie Rey's Ohana Grill (upscale casual)
- **Estimated food cost:** ~$150-200 (4 dinners + lunches + snacks)

### Safety:
- Ocean safety: check conditions before snorkeling
- Reef-safe sunscreen REQUIRED (regular sunscreen illegal in Hawaii!)
- Stay hydrated - bring water on hikes
- Cell service good in Kona area, spotty at Kiholo Bay
