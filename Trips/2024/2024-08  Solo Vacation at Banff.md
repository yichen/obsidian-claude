		- - **Sights:** Lake Louise, Moraine Lake, Peyto Lake Lookout, Bow Falls, Johnston Canyon, Lake Minnewanka, Columbia Icefields, Emerald Lake, Norquay Lookout, Takkakaw Falls
		- **Activities:** Banff Gondola, Banff Upper Hotsprings , drive the Icefield Parkway, paddle the Bow River, Sunshine Meadows, Horseback riding, sightseeing tours, Via Ferrata, rent an ebike
		- **Hikes:** Tunnel Mountain, Lake Agnes, Plains of Six Glaciers, Sulphur Mountain, Larch Valley/Citadel Pass, Stanley Glacier, Boom Lake

			- Runs every 20 min, cost is free for kids, $8 for adults, $4 seniors

### Thursday: Icefields Parkway

- As of August 9th, the Icefields Parkway is open between Lake Louise and the Athabasca Glacier area of the Columbia Icefield (Icefields Centre in the Athabasca)
- As of August 4th, road is only open until Saskatchewan River Crossing (1h 40m from Canmore, 1h 33m from Banff). Looks like Peyto Lake, Bow Lake are both accessible before the Saskatchewan River Crossing
- Hike Bow Glacier Falls
	- https://www.alltrails.com/ar/trail/canada/british-columbia/bow-glacier-falls
	- 5.5 mi distance, 787 ft gain.
	- Located along the infamous Icefields Parkway, this popular year-round excursion takes you to one of Banff's most accessible glaciers and a fantastic waterfall.
- Embark on a scenic drive along the Icefields Parkway, stopping at Peyto Lake, Bow Lake, and other stunning viewpoints.
- Visit the Columbia Icefield and take a glacier walk (optional).
	- Columbia Icefield is not accessible as it's 41 minutes north of the road closing at Saskatchewan River
- Enjoy lunch at one of the lodges along the parkway.
