## Day 3 — Wednesday, Apr 15: East Side Adventure (High-Energy Day)

**Theme:** Sunrise hike + scenic coast + beach

**Early Morning:**
- **6:00 AM — Drive to Diamond Head:** 10 min (2.5 miles)
  - [Diamond Head State Monument](https://dlnr.hawaii.gov/dsp/parks/oahu/diamond-head-state-monument/)
  - [Reserve at gostateparks.hawaii.gov](https://gostateparks.hawaii.gov/diamondhead)
  - Hours: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM (last entry 4:30 PM)
  - Cost: $5/person × 3 + $10 parking = **$25**
  - **Book the 6:00 AM slot** — coolest temps, least crowded, best light for photos
  - Trail: 1.6 miles round trip, 560 ft elevation gain
  - Allow 1–1.5 hours — steep stairs near summit, 8-year-olds can handle it
  - Bring water, sunscreen, hats — no shade on trail

> **⚠️ TIMED ENTRY REQUIRED:** Diamond Head reservations open **30 days in advance** at [gostateparks.hawaii.gov](https://gostateparks.hawaii.gov/diamondhead). For Apr 15, book starting **Mar 16**. $5/person entry + $10/vehicle parking. Credit card only.

- **7:30 AM** — Back at car. Snack break (granola bars, fruit from Costco shop card)

**Morning:**
- **8:00 AM — Drive to Makapuu Lighthouse Trail:** 20 min (10 miles) via Kalanianaole Hwy
  - Free, no reservation needed
  - Paved trail, 2 miles round trip — easy for kids, stroller-accessible
  - Whale watching possible in April (tail end of season)
  - Stunning views of Windward Coast, Rabbit Island, Manana Island
  - **Allow 1 hour**

- **9:30 AM — Drive to Lanikai Beach:** 15 min (7 miles)
  - One of Hawaii's best beaches — calm turquoise water, soft sand
  - Street parking only (limited) — arrive by 10 AM for parking
  - Great for kids — shallow, calm, no big waves
  - Mokulua Islands visible offshore
  - **Spend 2 hours** — swim, build sandcastles

**Afternoon:**
- **12:00 PM — Lunch in Kailua:** Buzz's Original Steakhouse or Kalapawai Market (5 min drive from Lanikai)
  - Budget: ~$40–50
- **1:00 PM — Drive back to hotel:** 30 min (15 miles) via Pali Hwy
- **1:30–4:30 PM — Hotel pool recovery**

**Evening:**
- **5:30 PM — Waikiki Beach sunset walk**
- **6:30 PM — Dinner: Musubi Cafe Iyasume** (2427 Kuhio Ave, 3 min walk)
  - Famous spam musubi, bento boxes — kid-friendly
  - Budget: ~$25–35

**Total driving: ~1 hr 15 min**
**Cost: $25 (Diamond Head) + ~$40 lunch + ~$30 dinner = ~$95**

## Safety Notes

### Ruby Sleepwalking Protocol
Per [[Trips/Lessons Learned|Lake Quinault 2025 incident]]:
- [ ] **Bring portable door alarm** — attach to hotel room door each night
- [ ] Nightlight plugged in near bathroom
- [ ] Brief kids on hotel room layout before first bedtime
- [ ] Yi sleeps nearest to the door
- [ ] Test door alarm on check-in night

### Sun & Water Safety
- [ ] **Reef-safe sunscreen ONLY** — required by Hawaii law (no oxybenzone/octinoxate)
  - Buy at Costco before trip or at any Hawaii drugstore
- [ ] Reapply sunscreen every 2 hours and after swimming
- [ ] Hats and rash guards for kids at all beaches
- [ ] Never swim without adult supervision
- [ ] At Waimea Bay: check lifeguard flags, stay in designated swim area
- [ ] At Hanauma Bay: stay in shallow reef area, watch for coral cuts

### Car Break-In Prevention
- [ ] **NEVER leave valuables visible in the car** — Oahu trailhead parking lots are targets
- [ ] At Makapuu, Lanikai, and North Shore: take everything or lock in trunk BEFORE arriving at the lot
- [ ] Consider bringing a steering wheel lock as visible deterrent
- [ ] Rental car = tourist target; park in well-trafficked areas

### General
- [ ] Download offline Google Maps for Oahu (cell signal can be spotty on North Shore)
- [ ] Save emergency numbers in phone (see Emergency Contacts below)
- [ ] Kids wear water shoes at Hanauma Bay (reef is sharp)
- [ ] Bring first aid kit for coral scrapes

## Lessons from Past Trips

Per [[Trips/Lessons Learned]] — most relevant lessons for this trip:

1. **Pool time is NOT filler — it's the highlight.** At Suncadia (Jan 2026), stress dropped from 80 to 28 in the pool with kids. Don't over-schedule. Every day has pool time built in. — [[Trips/2026/2026-01-23_25 Trip with Kids/2026-01-23 Trip Report for Suncadia|Suncadia 2026]]

2. **Ruby sleepwalking: door alarm is MANDATORY.** She exited the hotel room at Lake Quinault without waking anyone. Portable door alarm + nightlight + parent near door. — [[Trips/2025/2025-01-25 Lake Quinault Lodge|Lake Quinault 2025]]

3. **Multiple swimsuits required.** Need backups while one dries. Pack 2 per person minimum. — [[Trips/2025/2025-06-14 Great Wolf Lodge|Great Wolf Lodge 2025]]

4. **Don't overpack clothes.** 3 everyday + 3 hiking shirts was too much at Banff. For Hawaii beach trip, 4 shirts total per person is plenty. — [[Trips/2024/2024-08  Solo Vacation at Banff|Banff 2024]]

5. **Plan light activities on arrival day.** Even 3-hour drives require recovery. A 6-hour flight definitely does. Day 1 is hotel pool + sunset walk only. — [[Trips/2025/2025-10-08 Pothole State Park|Pothole 2025]]

6. **Actually check off the packing list.** Don't just read it — physically check items off. Drone, tech bag, and shower kit have all been forgotten before. — [[Trips/2025/2025-05-21 Coastal Oregon Road Trip|Oregon Coast 2025]]

7. **Hawaii-specific: Starlink Mini for dead zones.** Large areas of Oahu (especially North Shore) have poor cell signal. — [[Trips/2025/2025-07-31 Kauai Hawaii|Kauai 2025]]

## Weather & Driving Conditions

### April Weather on Oahu (Historical Averages)
- **High:** 82–84°F / **Low:** 70–72°F
- **Water temp:** 77°F — comfortable for swimming
- **Rain:** April is tail end of wet season; expect brief afternoon showers, especially on Windward side
- **Sunrise:** ~6:15 AM / **Sunset:** ~6:45 PM
- UV index: 10–11 (very high) — reapply sunscreen frequently

### Driving Conditions
- All roads are paved, well-maintained highways
- **H-1 traffic warning:** Rush hour (7–9 AM, 4–6 PM) on H-1 freeway can add 20–30 min. Itinerary avoids peak times.
- **North Shore (Day 5):** Kamehameha Hwy is a 2-lane road; can be slow behind tour buses. Leaving by 3:30 PM avoids the worst return traffic.
- **No weather hazards expected** for mid-April driving

> **Weather forecast will be checked 3 days before departure using [forecast.weather.gov](https://forecast.weather.gov/MapClick.php?lat=21.2783&lon=-157.8289)** per mandatory protocol.

- **Pearl Harbor:** National park (NPS), free with $1 reservation fee. [Book here](https://www.recreation.gov/ticket/facility/233338)
- **All other activities:** No permits/passes required
- **America the Beautiful Pass:** Not needed — Pearl Harbor is free; Diamond Head and Hanauma Bay are state/city managed (not federal)
