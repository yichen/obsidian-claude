## 2. Afternoon Activities (Wed-Fri, after morning work)

### Skiing

#### Bridger Bowl — LOCAL FAVORITE
- **Distance from downtown Bozeman:** 16 miles north, ~25 min drive
- **Lift ticket:** ~$30/day (non-profit ski area — remarkably affordable)
- **Terrain:** 2,000 acres, great intermediate and expert terrain
- **Season:** Dec through first weekend of April
- **Half-day option:** Available from 12:30 PM — perfect for afternoon skiing after work
- **Why March:** Good snow coverage, fewer crowds than Big Sky, locals' mountain
- **URL:** https://bridgerbowl.com/tickets-passes/tickets
- **Best for:** Afternoon half-day sessions. Short drive, cheap tickets, no crowds on weekdays.

#### Big Sky Resort
- **Distance from downtown Bozeman:** 45 miles south, ~1 hour drive via Gallatin Canyon (Hwy 191)
- **Lift ticket:** $98-$222/day (dynamic pricing); half-day from 12:30 PM at reduced rate
- **Terrain:** 5,850 acres — largest skiing in the US
- **Season:** Nov 26, 2025 – Apr 12, 2026
- **Why March:** 400+ inches annual snowfall, world-class terrain variety
- **URL:** https://www.bigskyresort.com/lift-tickets
- **Best for:** Full weekend day (Sat or Sun). The 1-hour drive makes it less ideal for afternoon half-days on work days. Save for a full weekend day.

### Museum of the Rockies
- **Distance:** On MSU campus, ~1.5 miles from downtown, 5 min drive
- **Hours:** Daily 9 AM - 5 PM
- **Admission:** Tickets sold on-site only; ~$16-18 adults (verify on arrival)
- **Highlights:** World-class dinosaur collection (one of largest T. rex skulls ever found), planetarium shows (extra fee), Living History Farm (May-Sep only)
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **URL:** https://museumoftherockies.org/visit
- **Best for:** Perfect afternoon activity, especially if weather is bad. One of the best dinosaur museums in the world.

### Hot Springs

#### Bozeman Hot Springs
- **Distance:** 8 miles west of downtown, ~15 min drive (81123 Gallatin Rd)
- **Hours:** Mon-Thu 6 AM-11 PM; Fri 6 AM-Sundown; Sat Sundown-11 PM; Sun 8 AM-11 PM
- **Weekday price:** $21 adult (pools only), $30 (pools + fitness)
- **Weekend price:** $28 adult (pools only)
- **Features:** 12 pools ranging 59-106°F, fitness center, sauna
- **URL:** https://bozemanhotsprings.co/prices/
- **Best for:** Weekday afternoon after work — $21, close to town, multiple temperature pools. Great stress relief per your travel preferences.

#### Chico Hot Springs Resort
- **Distance:** ~50 miles south via Paradise Valley, ~1 hour drive
- **Day pass:** $7.50 adult
- **Pools:** Two outdoor geothermal pools (96°F and 103°F)
- **Dining:** On-site restaurant (excellent reputation)
- **URL:** https://www.chicohotsprings.com/
- **Best for:** Combine with Paradise Valley scenic drive on a weekend day. The drive through Paradise Valley along the Yellowstone River is spectacular.

### Downtown Bozeman Walking
- **Distance:** 0 miles (you're there)
- **Main Street:** ~8 blocks of shops, galleries, restaurants, cafes
- **Highlights:** Local art galleries, bookstores, gear shops, coffee shops
- **Duration:** 1-2 hours browsing
- **Best for:** Quick afternoon walk between meetings or after early work end

### Winter Hiking (with microspikes)

#### Grotto Falls (Hyalite Canyon)
- **Distance:** ~20 miles south of Bozeman, 30 min drive
- **Trail:** 1.6 miles to waterfall, minimal elevation gain
- **Winter notes:** Road plowed to trailhead; bring microspikes
- **Duration:** 1-2 hours
- **Best for:** Short afternoon hike, beautiful frozen waterfall in March

#### Drinking Horse Mountain
- **Distance:** ~7 miles from downtown, 15 min drive (base of Bridger Canyon)
- **Trail:** 2.5 miles round trip, 700 ft elevation gain
- **Duration:** 1-1.5 hours
- **Best for:** Quick afternoon workout hike with views

#### Palisade Falls
- **Distance:** In Hyalite Canyon, ~20 miles south, 30 min drive
- **Trail:** Short, easy, paved in summer — icy in winter (microspikes essential)
- **Duration:** 30-60 min
- **Best for:** Quick waterfall visit, combine with Grotto Falls

## 3. Weekend Activities (Sat-Sun Full Days)

### Yellowstone National Park — Winter Access

**CRITICAL: Most of Yellowstone is CLOSED to regular vehicles in March.**

**What's open to cars:**
- North Entrance (Gardiner, MT) through to Cooke City via Tower Junction — this is the ONLY road open to regular vehicles year-round
- You can drive from Gardiner to Mammoth Hot Springs and on to Lamar Valley / Cooke City

**Over-snow vehicle season ending:**
- Mammoth to Norris: closes March 1
- Norris to Madison/Canyon: closes March 3
- Canyon to Fishing Bridge: closes March 8
- West/South/East entrances + remaining groomed roads: close March 15

**For your dates (Mar 11-15):** Over-snow travel is ending. By March 11, most interior roads are closed to snowcoaches. The North Entrance road remains open.

**Snowcoach Tours from Gardiner:**
- Operator: Yellowstone Vacation Tours, 905 Scott St W, Gardiner
- Tours to Old Faithful, Canyon, and custom routes
- Season: Dec 15, 2025 - ~March 1, 2026
- Price: ~$300-400/person full day
- Phone: 406-848-5171
- **NOTE:** Snowcoach tours may have already ended by March 11. Call to verify!
- URL: https://www.yellowstonevacations.com/tour/gardiner-snowcoach-tours/

**Self-Drive Option (North Entrance):**
- **Distance from Bozeman:** 85 miles to Gardiner via Paradise Valley (Hwy 89), ~1.5 hours
- **What you can see:** Mammoth Hot Springs, Lamar Valley (wildlife — bison, wolves, elk), Tower Fall area
- **Cost:** $35 vehicle entrance fee (or free with America the Beautiful Pass)
- **Duration:** Full day trip
- **Best for:** Saturday — combine with Chico Hot Springs on the way back
- URL: https://www.nps.gov/yell/planyourvisit/parkroads.htm

**PERMIT WARNING:** America the Beautiful Pass covers entrance fee. No timed entry required in winter.

### Big Sky Resort — Full Day Skiing
- 1-hour drive via Gallatin Canyon scenic drive
- Full day on 5,850 acres of terrain
- Advance purchase recommended for best pricing ($98-222 dynamic)
- **Best for:** Sunday — grand finale day

### Cross-Country Skiing / Snowshoeing

**Crosscut Mountain Sports Center**
- **Distance:** ~20 miles from Bozeman, in Bridger Canyon
- **Trails:** 28+ miles (45 km) groomed daily for skate and classic skiing
- **Snowshoeing:** 9 km of narrow gauge trails
- **Hours:** 9 AM - 4 PM daily
- **Rentals available:** Yes (skis, snowshoes)
- **Season:** December through March (may be late season by mid-March)
- **Phone:** (406) 586-9690
- **URL:** https://www.crosscutmt.org
- **Best for:** Peaceful morning or afternoon activity; great wildlife viewing

### Scenic Drives

#### Paradise Valley (Hwy 89)
- **Route:** Bozeman → Livingston (I-90 east, 25 min) → South on Hwy 89 through Paradise Valley → Gardiner (North Yellowstone entrance)
- **Distance:** ~85 miles one way, 1.5 hours
- **Highlights:** Yellowstone River, Absaroka Range views, ranches, hot springs
- **Combine with:** Chico Hot Springs stop ($7.50) + Yellowstone North Entrance drive-through
- **Best for:** Full day Saturday loop

#### Gallatin Canyon (Hwy 191)
- **Route:** Bozeman south on Hwy 191 toward Big Sky
- **Distance:** 45 miles to Big Sky, ~1 hour
- **Highlights:** Gallatin River, towering rock formations, deep forests, open valleys
- **Combine with:** Big Sky skiing or just a scenic out-and-back
- **Best for:** Drive to Big Sky skiing day

## 8. Driving Conditions Warning

**MANDATORY SAFETY NOTE:**
- March in Montana means possible snow, ice, and wind on all routes
- **Gallatin Canyon (Hwy 191 to Big Sky):** Narrow, winding canyon road. Can be icy. Check MDOT road conditions before driving
- **Bozeman Pass (I-90 to Livingston):** Can have whiteout conditions and high winds
- **Hyalite Canyon:** Road plowed but can be icy — 4WD/AWD recommended
- **Check conditions:** Montana DOT: https://www.511mt.net/ or call 511
- **Car rental:** Request AWD/4WD if available

## 10. Lessons from Past Trips

1. **Bring trekking poles** for winter hiking on icy trails — learned from Banff 2024 where wet/slippery trails were dangerous without them
2. **Bring rain pants** — consistently forgotten item; March Bozeman forecast shows rain/snow mix
3. **Microspikes essential** — frozen trails in Hyalite Canyon require traction
4. **Need to unplug from Slack 1 hour before leaving for activities** — learned from Joshua Tree 2025 workation to avoid guilt feeling
5. **Heated pools = significant stress relief** — Suncadia 2026 showed stress dropped from 80 to 28. Bozeman Hot Springs and Kimpton Armory rooftop pool are both options
6. **Bring trackpad instead of mouse** for laptop work — mouse slides off lap table (Silver Falls 2025)
7. **Actually CHECK OFF packing list items** — recurring mistake across multiple trips
8. **Don't overpack** — 3 everyday + 3 activity shirts is sufficient (Banff 2024)
