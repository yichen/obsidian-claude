# Trip Report: Irvine, CA Weekend
**Travel Party:** Date trip with Xiao Li (met on Bumble during my Joshua Tree National Park trip last year)
**Trip Dates:** January 16-18, 2026 (Friday-Sunday)
**Base:** Pasea Hotel & Spa, Huntington Beach

**Context:** Xiao Li was returning to China, possibly permanently. Saturday was her lunar calendar birthday and her last full day in the United States.

---

## Summary

A relaxed romantic weekend getaway in Orange County focused on personal connection and coastal exploration. The trip centered around Crystal Cove State Park with its pristine beach, rather than following the full planned itinerary. Weather was excellent - warmer than expected with sunny skies perfect for beach activities.

While Xiao Li felt very comfortable with me, I noticed relationship dynamics that didn't align with my preferences - particularly around expectations for small acts of service (asking me to hand her things she could easily reach, holding her bags). These may be cultural differences in how Chinese women express care or test investment, but they felt unnecessarily dependent to me. I managed not to complain since it was her last day in the US.

## What Actually Happened

### Friday: Brutal Travel Day
**Timeline:** 8:00 AM departure → 8:00 PM dinner finish

The travel day was far more complicated than past solo trips. I didn't tell my employers I was traveling, which led to a cascade of problems:

**Morning/Flight (8:00 AM - 2:40 PM):**
- Left home at 8:00 AM to catch flight
- Joined Slack team meeting at airport at 11:00 AM with flaky internet
- Meeting with Nikhil at ServiceTitan went badly due to connectivity issues
- Nikhil later complained that if he'd known I was traveling, we would have met the day before
- Landed at John Wayne Airport around 2:40 PM

**Afternoon Chaos (2:40 PM - 6:00 PM):**
- Ben noticed I seemed "out" (not active on Slack) and rescheduled Nikhil meeting to 4:30 PM
- Nikhil didn't show up for the rescheduled 4:30 PM call
- Rushed to hotel to check in around 4:00 PM to be ready for the call
- Drove to Irvine to pick up Xiao Li
- Returned to hotel past 6:00 PM

**Evening (6:00 PM - 8:00 PM):**
- Finished dinner close to 8:00 PM
- Total elapsed time from leaving home: 12 hours

**Lessons learned:** Travel is more complicated now with two jobs and a micromanaging manager (Ben). In the past with one job and a hands-off manager, solo travel was straightforward. Now I need to either:
1. Be transparent about travel days, or
2. Block calendar and set clear boundaries about availability

### Crystal Cove State Park Beach
**Visited:** Saturday (primary activity)

We spent most of our time at Crystal Cove State Park and took a very long walk on the beach. The 3.2-mile pristine coastline was perfect for a relaxed, unstructured day. The beach was relatively uncrowded compared to other Orange County beaches, allowing for quality conversation and connection time.

- Beautiful tide pools and coastal scenery
- Historic beach cottages made for great photo opportunities
- Long beach walk provided the natural, unhurried setting we were looking for
- **Parking:** $15 day-use fee (as expected - not covered by any pass)

**Evening at Pasea Hotel:**
After returning from the beach, we relaxed in the hotel hot tub and watched the sunset from there. The hot tub provided a perfect, romantic end to the day with ocean views and the warm glow of the setting sun over the Pacific.

### Laguna Beach - Skipped
**Attempted but did not visit**

We tried to visit Laguna Beach but found it too busy with no available parking. Rather than spending time circling for a spot, we decided to head back to Crystal Cove where we knew we could park and enjoy a less crowded experience.

**Lesson learned:** Weekend parking in Laguna Beach can be extremely difficult. Either arrive very early (before 10 AM) or skip it on busy weekends.

## What to Do Differently Next Time

### Travel Logistics:
⚠️ **Be transparent about travel days** - This was the first trip carrying **two laptops** for two jobs. Trying to hide travel from employers (especially with a micromanaging boss like Ben) creates cascading problems. Either inform them or block calendar completely
⚠️ **No work meetings on travel days** - The combination of airport wifi, flight logistics, and coordinating with a date made Friday exhausting and unprofessional
💡 **Block full travel day** - Consider taking PTO or blocking entire travel day rather than trying to juggle work obligations

### Destination Planning:
⚠️ **Laguna Beach parking** - If visiting on a weekend, arrive before 10 AM or consider weekday visits instead
⚠️ **Have backup plans** - Good to have alternatives when primary destinations are too crowded
💡 **Consider Crystal Cove for full day** - The beach is large enough and scenic enough to spend an entire day without needing other stops

### Relationship Observations:
⚠️ **Set boundaries early** - Small dependency behaviors (asking for things she could reach, bag-holding) emerged quickly. In future relationships, address these patterns early rather than accommodating them
⚠️ **Cultural expectations** - Recognize that some behaviors (like expecting men to hold bags) may be cultural norms for Chinese women, but clarify my own preferences upfront
⚠️ **Projection vs. Reality (Putney's Theory)** - This was a clear case of Putney's "Love as a Projection." I projected my own values (education, intellect) onto her based on superficial markers (wealth, travel history). I fell for my own ideal image, not the real person. **Lesson:** Stop filling in the blanks with positive assumptions. Observe the person as they are.
💡 **Last-day context** - Being accommodating worked for a farewell situation, but this wouldn't be sustainable in an ongoing relationship

## Recommendations for Future Trips

### If Returning to Orange County:
- **Crystal Cove State Park** - Highly recommend, worth a full day
- **Avoid Laguna Beach on weekends** - Too crowded, parking nightmare
- **Pasea Hotel & Spa** - Great base for Orange County coastal access. Expensive, $60/day valet parking.

### For Similar Date Weekends:
- Less structured itineraries work better for connection time
- One or two primary destinations is better than trying to hit multiple spots
- Beach walks provide natural conversation opportunities without feeling forced
- Early dates reveal compatibility around independence vs. dependency dynamics - pay attention to these signals

## Notes

This trip succeeded by embracing flexibility. While the original plan included multiple destinations (Newport Beach, Laguna Beach, Crystal Cove), focusing our energy on one beautiful location - Crystal Cove - created a more relaxed and enjoyable experience than rushing between crowded beaches would have.
