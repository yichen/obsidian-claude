# 2026-02-01 [PLAN] Olympic Peninsula Solo Trip

## High-Level Plan
- **Leave:** Sunday, Feb 1 @ 9:00 AM
- **Return:** Monday, Feb 2 @ 2:30 PM (Exchange @ 3:00 PM)
- **Primary Focus:** Rainforest hiking, coastal photography, and stress relief.
- **Estimated Cost:** $250 - $400 (Lodge + Meals + Gas)

## Sunday - Feb 1: Rainforest Immersion
- **Morning (9:00 AM):** Depart Sammamish for Quinault Rainforest.
- **Midday (12:30 PM):** Arrive Quinault Rainforest.
- **Activity:** [Quinault Rainforest Nature Trail](https://www.wta.org/go-hiking/hikes/quinault-rainforest-nature-trail)
    - **Drive from home:** 3 hours 15 mins (165 miles)
    - **Trail:** 0.5 mile loop (Easy, 30-45 mins). Beautiful mossy trees and creeks.
- **Late Afternoon (3:00 PM):** Check-in at [Kalaloch Lodge](https://www.thekalalochlodge.com/).
    - **Drive from Quinault:** 45 mins (35 miles)
- **Evening:** 
    - Visit **Tree of Life** (located just north of the lodge on the beach).
    - Sunset at **Kalaloch Beach** (Sunset @ 5:15 PM).
    - Dinner at **Creekside Restaurant** (Reservations recommended: 360-962-2271).

## Monday - Feb 2: Coastal Photography & Return
- **Early Morning (7:30 AM):** Visit **Ruby Beach** for low tide.
    - **Low Tide:** ~6:30 AM (2.7 ft). Early morning is best for tide pools before the 12:30 PM high tide.
    - **Drive from Lodge:** 10 mins (8 miles)
- **Morning (11:00 AM):** Depart for home.
- **Return (2:30 PM):** Arrive Sammamish.
    - **Drive from Ruby Beach:** 3 hours 30 mins (175 miles)

---

## Key Logistics
**Total Driving:** ~7.5 hours round trip.
**Passes/Permits:** 
- **Olympic National Park Pass:** Required (or America the Beautiful Pass).
- **Makah Permit:** Not required for this area (only for Neah Bay).

## Weather & Safety
- **Forecast:** Cloudy with light rain (45-55°F).
- **Driving Conditions:** Generally clear but expect wet roads. Check [WSDOT Stevens Pass](https://wsdot.com/travel/real-time/mountainpasses/stevens) if taking a northern route, but US-101 is the standard path.

## Standard Itinerary Components
- **Hotel:** [Kalaloch Lodge](https://www.thekalalochlodge.com/) (Standard Lodge Room or Cabin).
- **Alternative:** If Kalaloch is full, [Lake Quinault Lodge](https://www.olympicnationalparks.com/lodging/lake-quinault-lodge/) is the best backup.

---

## Lessons from Past Trips
1. **Stress Relief:** Focus on the ocean sounds; it has a similar de-stressing effect to the pool last week.
2. **Packing:** Use your visual checklist. Don't forget rain pants!
3. **Food:** Creekside Restaurant in the lodge is usually great, but call ahead for a table.
