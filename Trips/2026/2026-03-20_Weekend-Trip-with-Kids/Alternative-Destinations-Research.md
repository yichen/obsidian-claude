## Option 1: Enzian Inn, Leavenworth, WA — TOP RECOMMENDATION

### Drive
- **From Sammamish:** ~2 hours via I-90 W to US-2 E (through Stevens Pass area)
- **Distance:** ~110 miles

### Hotel & Pool
- **Enzian Inn** — Bavarian-themed family hotel, highly rated for families
- **Indoor pool:** Year-round heated indoor pool (5 feet deep throughout, steps for entry)
- **Outdoor pools:** Two outdoor heated pools including a shallow kids' pool
- **Hot tubs:** Four spa tubs (indoor and outdoor)
- **Pool hours:** 8:00 AM - 10:00 PM
- **Pool quality rating: ★★★★** — Multiple pools, hot tubs, and a dedicated kids' pool. Not a waterpark, but the variety of pools (indoor + outdoor + hot tubs) gives the kids plenty of variety. Reviews confirm kids love it.
- **Bonus:** Indoor sports court, Championship putting course
- **Breakfast:** Complimentary hot buffet breakfast with live alphorn performance

### Cost
- **~$280-360/night** (March pricing; weekends are higher)
- **Total weekend estimate:** ~$600-750 for 2 nights

### Kid-Friendly Activities (Saturday)
- **Leavenworth Bavarian Village:** Walk the charming village, candy shops, toy stores, German bakeries
- **Leavenworth Ski Hill / Tubing:** 100-foot tubing hill with rope tow, tubes on site, hot cocoa at the lodge ($15-20/person). Current snow: 8 inches packed powder, conditions are rideable.
- **Sledding Hill:** At Leavenworth Golf Course — groomed hill, bring your own sled or rent tubes
- **Reindeer Farm:** CLOSED in March (birthing season) — skip this
- **Waterfront Park:** Walk along the Wenatchee River
- **Nutcracker Museum:** Small but unique, kids enjoy the quirky collection

### Dining
- **Rhein Haus:** Bavarian grill, char-broiled sausages, warm pretzels — great for kids
- **Louies:** Best breakfast spot (cornflake-fried chicken waffles)
- **South:** Tex-Mex, fast service, kid-friendly
- **The Soup Cellar:** Cozy, family-oriented
- **Leavenworth Sausage Garten:** Quick outdoor sausages with toppings

### Weather (March 20-22)
- **Friday 3/20:** High 61F, rain likely (60% chance) — drive in rain, pool evening
- **Saturday 3/21:** High 52F, mostly sunny (20% rain before 11am) — great activity day
- **Sunday 3/22:** High 53F, mostly sunny — easy drive home

### March Considerations
- Snow tubing likely available but thin snowpack (39% of normal). Call Leavenworth Ski Hill to confirm: (509) 548-5477
- Village shops and restaurants fully open
- Lower crowds than summer/Christmas seasons
- Stevens Pass drive: check WSDOT conditions, chains may be required on US-2

### Why This Works
The Enzian Inn pool setup (indoor + outdoor + 4 hot tubs + kids' pool) is the closest hotel-pool experience to what Sol Duc would have offered. Combined with snow tubing and the Bavarian village, this gives the kids a genuinely exciting weekend with diverse activities. The pool is a real highlight here, not an afterthought.

## Option 2: Harrison Hot Springs Resort, BC — BEST POOL

### Drive
- **From Sammamish:** ~3-3.5 hours via I-5 N to Sumas border crossing, then Hwy 1 E
- **Distance:** ~150 miles + border crossing time (15-45 min)

### Hotel & Pool
- **Harrison Hot Springs Resort** — Natural hot springs resort on Harrison Lake
- **5 mineral hot springs pools** (2 indoor, 3 outdoor):
  - **Indoor Crescent Pool:** 32C/90F, calm soaking pool
  - **Indoor circular hot pool:** Deep relaxation pool with glass waterfall, 30-foot vaulted ceilings
  - **Outdoor family pool:** ~4,000 sq ft, natural hot springs setting with waterfalls, granite rocks, lush foliage, **zero-entry sloped entrance** (beach-style) perfect for kids
  - **Outdoor lap pool:** 30C/87F
  - **Adults-only pool:** 40C/105F
- **Pool quality rating: ★★★★★** — This is the closest experience to Sol Duc Hot Springs. Natural mineral water, multiple temperature options, the outdoor family pool with waterfalls and zero-entry is spectacular. This IS the activity, not just an amenity.
- **NOTE:** Outdoor water park on the lake is seasonal (June-September), closed in March

### Cost
- **~$150-230 CAD/night** (~$110-170 USD at current exchange rates) — surprisingly affordable
- **Total weekend estimate:** ~$300-460 CAD ($220-340 USD) for 2 nights

### Kid-Friendly Activities (Saturday)
- **Hot springs pools** — the main event, kids could spend hours
- **Harrison Lake beach walk**
- **Hiking trails** around the resort
- **Hell's Gate Airtram** — ride above Fraser Canyon (check seasonal availability)
- **Village exploration** — small lakeside town

### Dining
- Three on-site restaurants at the resort
- In-room pizza service available
- Small village with additional options

### Weather (March 20-22)
- Similar to Seattle-area weather: highs ~48-52F, rain likely Friday, improving Saturday-Sunday
- The outdoor pools are heated by hot springs so usable in any weather

### March Considerations
- **Border crossing required:** You have a Green Card, which is fine. Kids need passports or birth certificates + proof of citizenship. Confirm you have proper docs.
- **Drive time is the main drawback:** 3-3.5h each way with border crossing adds up for a 2-night trip
- **Exchange rate advantage:** Canadian dollar is favorable, making this very affordable
- **Seasonal closures:** Water park and some lake activities closed in March. Hot springs pools are year-round.

### Why This Works (and Why It Might Not)
This has the BEST pool experience of any option — actual hot springs with 5 pools, outdoor family pool with waterfalls and zero-entry, and it's the true spiritual successor to Sol Duc. However, the 3-3.5h drive + border crossing makes this tight for a Friday evening departure. You would likely need to leave work by 2 PM to arrive by dinner. If you can swing an early departure, this is the winner for pool quality.

## Option 3: Tulalip Resort Casino, Marysville, WA — EASIEST OPTION

### Drive
- **From Sammamish:** ~45 minutes via I-405 N to I-5 N
- **Distance:** ~40 miles

### Hotel & Pool
- **Tulalip Resort Casino** — Luxury resort with full amenities
- **Oasis Pool:** Indoor heated pool under a soaring glass atrium
  - Cascading waterfall feature into the pool
  - Footbridge over the pool
  - Hot tub (indoor)
  - Seasonal outdoor patio
  - Towel service
- **Pool hours:** 7 AM - 11 PM (adult-only 9-11 PM)
- **Pool quality rating: ★★★☆** — The glass atrium and waterfall make it feel special, and it's well-maintained. But it's a single pool with a hot tub — not the multi-pool variety the kids would get at other options. Good but not a highlight destination.

### Cost
- **~$170-275/night** (varies by date)
- **Total weekend estimate:** ~$350-550 for 2 nights

### Kid-Friendly Activities (Saturday)
- **Cabela's:** Giant outdoor store, kids love the aquariums and animal displays — free
- **Seattle Premium Outlets:** 130+ stores, indoor food court
- **Imagine Children's Museum** (Everett, ~20 min south): Interactive exhibits for kids
- **Funko HQ Store:** Pop culture collectibles, kids love browsing
- **Hibulb Cultural Center:** Local tribal history, educational

### Dining
- Multiple on-site restaurants at the resort
- Ram Restaurant & Brewery at the outlets
- Chain restaurants nearby (it's a commercial area)

### Weather (March 20-22)
- **Friday 3/20:** High 58F, rain (90% chance)
- **Saturday 3/21:** High 51F, rain clearing by midday
- **Sunday 3/22:** High 51F, partly sunny with chance of rain

### March Considerations
- Everything is open year-round (indoor mall, Cabela's, resort)
- This is a commercial/casino area, not a scenic getaway
- Short drive means you can leave later Friday and still have a full evening at the pool

### Why This Works (and Why It Might Not)
The 45-minute drive is unbeatable — you could leave after work Friday and be in the pool by 6 PM. The glass atrium pool with waterfall is nice. But this feels more like a "staycation at a nice hotel" than a weekend adventure. The surrounding area is a mall/casino zone, not a charming village or natural setting. The kids might not feel like they "went somewhere." Best as a fallback if weather is terrible or you want minimal driving.

## Option 4: Alderbrook Resort & Spa, Hood Canal — MOST SCENIC

### Drive
- **From Sammamish:** ~2 hours via I-5 S to Hwy 101 N (through Shelton)
- **Distance:** ~100 miles

### Hotel & Pool
- **Alderbrook Resort & Spa** — Waterfront resort on Hood Canal
- **Heated saltwater indoor pool** in a glass building overlooking the canal and beach
  - When tide is up, water comes right up to the pool building — stunning views
  - Floor-to-ceiling windows
  - Hot tub
  - Sauna and steam room
- **Pool hours:** 5 AM - 12 AM daily
- **Kids swim:** 9 AM - 9 PM (adult-only 5-9 AM and 9 PM-12 AM)
- **Pool quality rating: ★★★** — The setting is gorgeous (saltwater pool with floor-to-ceiling windows on Hood Canal), but it's a single pool + hot tub. Beautiful and relaxing, but not the multi-pool/water-features excitement that would replace Sol Duc for 8-year-olds. This pool is more impressive for adults than kids.

### Cost
- **~$250-320/night** (March is actually lower season here)
- **Total weekend estimate:** ~$500-650 for 2 nights

### Kid-Friendly Activities (Saturday)
- **Beach exploration:** Private beach access, tide pools, shell collecting
- **Hiking:** 8 miles of trails on the 84-acre property
- **Kayaks/paddleboards:** Rentable from the outdoor center (weather-dependent in March)
- **Game room:** Xbox, board games, books in the lodge
- **S'mores:** Nightly at 5 PM, complimentary kits from front desk, waterfront firepit
- **Geocaching:** Multiple caches on the property
- **Twanoh State Park:** 10 minutes away, beach access
- **Lady Alderbrook cruises:** $20/adult, $10/kid (check March availability)

### Dining
- On-site restaurant with Hood Canal views
- Limited off-site options (rural area — Union is a tiny town)

### Weather (March 20-22)
- **Friday 3/20:** High 55F, rain (90% chance). Flood Watch in effect.
- **Saturday 3/21:** High 56F, rain (90% chance)
- **Sunday 3/22:** High 50F, partly sunny, chance of rain

### March Considerations
- **Weather concern:** Hood Canal is getting the heaviest rain of all options this weekend. Two full days of rain at 90% chance limits outdoor activities significantly.
- Water sports (kayaks, paddleboards) unlikely in March rain
- The resort itself is cozy in rain (fireplace, game room, pool), but you'll feel cabin-bound
- Rural location means no backup entertainment if weather is bad

### Why This Works (and Why It Might Not)
Alderbrook is genuinely beautiful and relaxing, with one of the most scenic pool settings in the PNW. The s'mores, beach walks, hiking trails, and game room round it out. However, for THIS trip where the pool needs to be a highlight for 8-year-olds replacing Sol Duc, it falls short — one pool and a hot tub, however pretty, won't keep the twins as excited as multiple pools/hot tubs at Enzian Inn or actual hot springs at Harrison. Additionally, the forecast shows very heavy rain for Hood Canal this weekend, which would limit outdoor activities.

## RECOMMENDATION

### Top Pick: Enzian Inn, Leavenworth

**Why:** Best overall balance of pool quality, kid activities, drive time, and weekend experience.

1. **Pool:** 4 hot tubs + indoor pool + 2 outdoor pools + kids' pool = closest hotel-pool experience to replacing Sol Duc. Multiple pools keep kids entertained and exploring.
2. **Activities:** Snow tubing Saturday morning + Bavarian village walkabout + pool time = a full, diverse weekend. The kids get snow AND pool.
3. **Charm factor:** Leavenworth's Bavarian village feels like "going somewhere special" — it's not a mall or a generic resort.
4. **Weather:** Saturday and Sunday look good (mostly sunny, 52-53F). Friday rain is fine since you'll be driving and then hitting the pool.
5. **Practical:** 2-hour drive is manageable for a Friday afternoon departure.

### If You Can Leave Work Early Friday: Harrison Hot Springs

If you can depart by 2 PM to arrive for dinner, Harrison is the pool winner. Five natural mineral hot springs pools with an outdoor family pool featuring waterfalls and a zero-entry beach entrance — this is the true Sol Duc replacement. But the 3-3.5h drive + border makes it tight.

### Quick & Easy Backup: Tulalip Resort

If weather turns terrible or plans change last minute, Tulalip is 45 minutes away with a nice indoor pool, Cabela's for the kids, and outlet shopping. Not adventurous, but reliable.

## Lessons from Past Trips

Per [[Trips/Lessons Learned.md]]:

1. **Pack slippers for everyone** — lodge doesn't provide them, kids are slow putting on shoes returning from pool (Suncadia 2026)
2. **Bring multiple swimsuits** per person — need backups while one dries (Great Wolf Lodge 2025)
3. **Heated pools = proven stress reducer** — stress dropped from 80 to 28 at Suncadia (Suncadia 2026)
4. **Ruby sleepwalking risk** — use door alarm, nightlight, brief kids on room layout, parent near door (Lake Quinault 2025)
5. **Plan light activities on arrival day** — even 2h drives need recovery time (Pothole 2025)

## Driving Conditions Warning

- **Leavenworth via US-2:** Check WSDOT for Stevens Pass conditions. Chains may be required in March. [WSDOT Pass Report](https://wsdot.com/travel/real-time/mountainpasses)
- **Hood Canal:** Flood Watch in effect through Friday 3/20 5 PM. Monitor conditions if choosing Alderbrook.
- **Harrison Hot Springs:** I-5 to Sumas border. No mountain passes, but border wait times vary. Check [CBSA wait times](https://www.cbsa-asfc.gc.ca/bwt-taf/menu-eng.html).

## Pass/Permit Check

- **Leavenworth:** No passes required. Tubing hill charges per session (~$15-20).
- **Tulalip:** No passes required.
- **Alderbrook:** No passes required. Twanoh State Park nearby requires **Discover Pass** ($30/year or $10/day).
- **Harrison Hot Springs:** Valid passport or passport card required for border crossing. Green Card for you; kids need US passports or birth certificates.
