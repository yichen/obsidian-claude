## Day-by-Day Itinerary

**If staying at Sol Duc Resort:**

**If staying in Port Angeles:**

- Dinner in Port Angeles:
  - **Next Door Gastropub** — best all-around, kid-friendly
  - **Granny's Cafe** — casual family dining
  - **Westside Pizza** — 612 S Lincoln St, 11am-9pm (known from past trip)

**Evening:** Brief kids on hotel room layout (Ruby sleepwalking protocol). Set up door alarm and nightlight.

### Day 2 — Saturday, March 21: Sol Duc + Exploration

**Weather:** 48F, rain before 11 AM, **partly sunny afternoon** (best day)

**Morning (rain expected — do sheltered activities):**

**8:00 AM** — Breakfast

**9:00 AM — Sol Duc Falls Trail**
- **Drive from Port Angeles:** 40 min (32 miles) via US-101 W → Sol Duc Road
- **Trail:** 1.6 miles round trip, 200 ft elevation gain, easy
- Stunning waterfall through old-growth forest
- Duration: 1-1.5 hours
- Kid-friendly: Yes — well-maintained, short, exciting payoff
- NPS info: https://www.nps.gov/olym/planyourvisit/sol-duc-valley.htm

**10:30 AM — Ancient Groves Nature Trail** (nearby)
- 0.6-mile flat loop through massive old-growth trees
- 10-min drive from Sol Duc Falls trailhead
- Duration: 30-45 min
- Great for kids — giant trees are impressive

**Afternoon (clearing weather):**

**11:30 AM - 1:00 PM** — Sol Duc Hot Springs
- Four mineral pools at 98-104°F
- $19/adult, $15/child per 90-min session, $5 towel rental
- If staying at resort: free access, walk over anytime
- If day visit: first-come-first-served, no reservation needed
- **Spring hours:** 7:30 AM - 9:00 PM
- Kids will love the warm pools — proven stress reliever (Suncadia lesson: stress dropped 80→28)

**2:00 PM — Option A: Marymere Falls** (if weather clears)
- **Drive from Sol Duc:** 35 min back toward Port Angeles, then 20 min to Lake Crescent
- **Trail:** 1.8 miles round trip, 500 ft elevation, easy/moderate
- 90-foot waterfall through old-growth forest
- Duration: 1-1.5 hours
- Near Storm King Ranger Station on Lake Crescent

**2:00 PM — Option B: Hurricane Ridge** (if weather is clear + road is open)
- **Drive from Sol Duc:** ~1 hour via Port Angeles
- **Road open:** Fri-Sun 9 AM - 4 PM (call ahead: 360-565-3131)
- Possible snow at 5,242 ft — kids can play in snow!
- **Chains required** in vehicle
- Stunning views of the Olympic range on clear days
- Duration: 1-2 hours
- **Decision point:** Call the morning of. If clear skies, go for it. If socked in, skip (no views).

**5:00 PM** — Return to accommodation

**Evening:**
- Second hot springs soak if at Sol Duc Resort
- Dinner in Port Angeles if staying there:
  - **Kokopelli Grill** — nicer dinner option
  - **Downriggers** — waterfront seafood
- Set up sleepwalking safety measures again

### Day 3 — Sunday, March 22: New Experience + Drive Home

**Weather:** 49F, chance rain, partly sunny

**Option A: Hoh Rain Forest (top recommendation)**
- **Drive from Port Angeles:** 1h 45min (91 miles) via US-101 W
- **Drive from Sol Duc area:** 1h 30min
- **Trails:**
  - Hall of Mosses — 0.8 miles, flat, easy, magical for kids
  - Spruce Nature Trail — 1.2 miles, flat, easy
- Duration: 2-2.5 hours for both trails
- **This is the iconic ONP experience you haven't done with the kids** (trails were closed Jan 2025!)
- Visitor Center open Fri-Sun 9 AM - 4 PM
- NPS info: https://www.nps.gov/olym/planyourvisit/visiting-the-hoh.htm

**Hoh itinerary:**
- 8:00 AM — Depart accommodation
- 9:45 AM — Arrive Hoh Rain Forest
- 10:00-12:00 PM — Hike Hall of Mosses + Spruce Nature Trail
- 12:00 PM — Head home

**Option B: Dungeness Spit (new experience, closer to home route)**
- **Drive from Port Angeles:** 25 min east (toward home)
- Longest natural sand spit in the US (5.5 miles) — walk as far as you want
- $3/family entry (Dungeness National Wildlife Refuge)
- Duration: 1-2 hours
- Good for a relaxed Sunday morning — on the way home

**Option C: Salt Creek Tidepools + WWII Bunkers (new, free)**
- **Drive from Port Angeles:** 25 min west
- Free tidepools at low tide + old WWII gun bunkers kids can explore
- Check tide tables: https://tides.net/washington/714/
- Duration: 1-1.5 hours

**12:00-1:00 PM** — Depart for home
- Stop in Sequim or Port Townsend for lunch
- **Olympic Game Farm** (Sequim, 25 min from PA) — drive-through safari, $25/adult, $20/child — fun final stop if time allows
- Ferry from Kingston → Edmonds
- **Arrive home:** ~4:00-5:00 PM
- **Lesson:** Skip evening plans on return day — everyone needs recovery time
