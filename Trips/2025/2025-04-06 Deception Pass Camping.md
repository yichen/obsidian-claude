## Major Incident: Lost Tent

**What Happened:**
- Packed tent and placed on top of car
- Got distracted by other tasks and forgot to tie it down
- Tent fell off during drive
- Didn't notice until 15 minutes into drive
- **Decision:** Didn't go back to look for it
- **Regret:** Should have gone back - would have been valuable lesson for kids about doing your best instead of giving up early

**Laurence's Suggestion:** Go back and look for it (I said no)

**Lesson Learned:**
- ⚠️ **Never put items on car roof without immediately securing them**
- ⚠️ **When kids suggest doing the right thing, listen to them**
- 💡 **Teaching moment missed:** Should have modeled persistence and problem-solving
- 💡 **Create checklist for loading car** - visual reminder to secure all items

## ADHD-Specific Camping Strategies

### Bathroom Protocol
✅ **New Rule:** When ONE twin needs bathroom, BOTH go
- Don't wait for them to recognize their own needs
- Proactive prevention of accidents
- Build in regular bathroom breaks (every 2-3 hours)

### Packing Protocol
✅ **Create Visual Checklists:**
1. Make laminated packing list for each child
2. Include pictures for younger kids
3. Check off items together as they pack
4. Parent final verification required
5. Don't assume they remembered everything

### Loading Car Protocol
✅ **Never Multi-Task During Loading:**
1. If item goes on roof → secure IMMEDIATELY
2. Don't get distracted by other tasks
3. Visual checklist: "Is everything tied down?"
4. Walk around car before leaving
5. Get kids to help verify everything is secure (teaches responsibility)

## Parenting Lessons

### What Went Wrong
❌ Got distracted during car loading (lost tent)
❌ Didn't go back to look for tent (gave up too early)
❌ Missed teaching moment about persistence
❌ Didn't listen to Laurence's good suggestion

### What to Do Differently
✅ **When kids suggest the right thing → do it**
- Laurence wanted to go back and look
- I should have listened and made it a learning experience
- Even if we didn't find it, the effort matters

✅ **Model persistence, not giving up**
- "We'll go back and look" teaches problem-solving
- "It's probably gone" teaches defeat
- Kids learn more from our actions than words

✅ **Turn mistakes into teaching moments**
- Lost tent = lesson about securing items
- Could have been lesson about persistence
- Could have been lesson about retracing steps
