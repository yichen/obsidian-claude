## Trip Report - Solo April 2025

### Weather & Conditions
- **Wind:** Very windy - not comfortable for ground tents
- **Temperature:** Cold evenings and mornings
- **Bugs:** Some bugs and mosquitos present during sunset when sitting outside
- **Overall:** Low temperatures reduced motivation for hiking

### What Worked

#### Heated Jacket Success
✅ **Costco heated jacket**
- Lightweight
- Provides excellent warmth for evening/morning coldness
- Highly recommend for spring camping

#### Car Camping
✅ Slept in car (no tent needed)
- Practical given windy conditions
- Protected from elements

### What Didn't Work

#### Power Management
⚠️ **Small Anker battery insufficient:**
- Lasted only one night
- Used for Starlink 6:30 PM - 10:00 PM
- Had 20% remaining
- **Good news:** Brought two extra batteries (1280Wh each)
  - Each should power Starlink (40W) for 20+ hours

#### Comfort Issues
❌ **Long-time sitting in car is not comfortable**
- Need better setup for extended car camping
- Consider camp chairs or better in-car arrangement

#### Shower Situation
❌ **No easy shower access**
- Reduced motivation for hiking
- Low temperature made it worse
- **Future solution:** Bring shower tent (when wind allows)

### Food & Sleep

#### Intermittent Fasting Experience
- Only ate dinner: Full-length Safeway sandwich
- Worried about hunger and sleep problems
- **Result:** Struggled to fall asleep initially, but managed to sleep in after early wake-up
- Fasting while camping is doable but affects sleep quality

## Lessons Learned

### Car Camping
✅ **Slept in car worked well for windy conditions**
❌ **Long sitting uncomfortable** - need better seating arrangement
💡 **Bring camp chairs** even when car camping

### Power Planning
⚠️ **Small Anker battery insufficient for overnight Starlink use**
✅ **Larger batteries (1280Wh) essential** for extended power needs
💡 **Calculate power needs before trip** - Starlink uses ~40W

### Weather Considerations
⚠️ **Very windy** - ground tents and shower tents not practical
⚠️ **Cold temperatures** reduce motivation for activities
💡 **Check wind forecast** before deciding tent vs. car camping

### Comfort & Hygiene
❌ **No easy shower** reduces appeal of multi-day stay
💡 **Shower tent setup** only viable in low-wind conditions
💡 **Consider heated shower tent** for cold spring weather

### Food Strategy
⚠️ **Intermittent fasting affects sleep** when camping
💡 **May need to eat more** when camping vs. home

## Recommendations for Future Trips

### For Solo Trips
- [ ] Bring larger power station (1280Wh minimum for Starlink overnight)
- [ ] Pack heated jacket (Costco one is excellent)
- [ ] Bring comfortable camp chair (don't rely on car seats)
- [ ] Check wind forecast before committing to tent camping
- [ ] Consider eating schedule - fasting may affect sleep

### For Trips with Kids
- [ ] Use trailer with roof-top tent (not ground tent)
- [ ] Bring mountain bikes for kids
- [ ] Check South Gate status or plan for North Gate route
- [ ] Bring extra warm layers for cold mornings/evenings
- [ ] Consider spring timing carefully (still quite cold)

### Ideal Setup for This Location
1. **Roof-top tent** on trailer (flat ground not needed, protected from wind)
2. **Large power station** (1280Wh+) for electronics
3. **Heated jacket** for temperature comfort
4. **Camp chairs** for outdoor sitting
5. **Mountain bikes** for family activity
6. **Check wind forecast** - shower tent only if calm
