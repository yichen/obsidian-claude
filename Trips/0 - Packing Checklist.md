## Conditional Packing (Based on Trip Type)

**Check these conditions and pack accordingly:**

### If: Kids + Swimming (Pool, Beach, Resort)
- [ ] Youth swim goggles (2x) - kids need these for any swimming activity
- [ ] Slippers for each person (lodge doesn't provide, kids slow putting on shoes)
- [ ] Multiple swimsuits per person (need backup while one dries)

### If: Kids + Hotel Stay
- [ ] Door alarm or security measure (Ruby sleepwalking incident)
- [ ] Nightlight
- [ ] Brief kids on room layout before bed, parent near door

### If: Solo Hiking Trip
- [ ] Folding trekking poles (longer length preferred)
- [ ] Electrolyte/carbohydrate pills (fasted hiking causes dizziness)
- [ ] Rain pants (not just umbrella)
- [ ] Long sleeve base layer (especially October+)
- [ ] Toe socks for hiking comfort
- [ ] Synthetic/wool shirts only (no cotton)
- [ ] MAX: 3 everyday shirts + 3 hiking shirts

### If: October/Fall Mountain Hiking
- [ ] Long sleeve base layer (always)
- [ ] Rain pants

### If: Hawaii Trip
- [ ] Starlink Mini (large areas with no cell signal)
- [ ] Hiking spikes for slippery mud
- [ ] Larger water pack (for 8+ mile hikes)
- [ ] Hiking poles only if trek >6 miles

### If: Shoulder Season Camping (Spring/Fall)
- [ ] Portable heater (Mr. Heater Buddy)
- [ ] Heated jacket (Costco)
- [ ] Plan for evening temps (70s day → 40s evening)

### If: Family Camping with Kids
- [ ] Laminated packing list with pictures for each child
- [ ] Three camp chairs (one per person)
- [ ] Kids' slippers
- [ ] Garbage bags in kids' shoe bags (rain protection)

### If: China Trip
- [ ] Soft-shell bag (not hard suitcase) for domestic travel
- [ ] 3C-certified power bank (required for domestic flights)
- [ ] Kindle (no WiFi on Shenzhen/Tibet Airlines)
- [ ] Plan 5 days for jet lag recovery

### If: Workation/Remote Work
- [ ] Starlink + large battery (1280Wh+ for overnight)
- [ ] Trackpad instead of mouse (slides off lap table)
- [ ] iPhone holder for monitor (video calls)
- [ ] Permanent tech cable bag

### If: Road Trip (3+ hours)
- [ ] In-car pee bag
- [ ] Clothing lines for wet clothes
- [ ] Plan light activities on arrival day (recovery needed)

### If: Tent Camping
- [ ] Memory foam topper (3-inch mattress too stiff)
- [ ] Compact lighter (even if buying firewood)
- [ ] Plastic grocery bags (garbage bags)
- [ ] Cardboard box (firewood transport → garbage container)

## Stay with Hotels and Airbnbs
#packing/hotel

- Bring rain paints for nature destination (Bring umbrella for city destination) if there is a chance of rain. I didn't bring my rain pants for Banff, and ended up having to buy an umbrella. 
- Bring travel wifi router. If I stay at one place more than a day, I just need to connect the router to wifi once through the app, and all my devices are already connected to the Chen-Travel wifi network.
- If I need to park in a garage of an airbnb, do not bring roof top cargo box. At my Canmore airbnb, the garage clearance is 6'1, and my Acura MDX can barely go through. 
- Bring my water-proof sneakers. They are comfortable to walk around in rain.
- Bring the lightweight hiking boots for anywhere with hiking, because the ankle support is important.
- **Bring the portable standing desk** if I am traveling by car and will stay with a place with WiFi
- Photography
	- Use iPhone if I need other people to take my photos.
	- Leica Q2 is not good enough for videos, because the videos are choppy. Use Sony FX3, or iPhone instead.


## Stay in a tent or car
#packing/carcamping
- Bring the folding camp chair. I will need to sit outside of the car or tent for comfort. I regretted not bringing the folding chair in my Banff trip.
- Bring wood to help level the car on the ground with slope. I am glad I did at Mora Campground, but I always forget them when leaving
- Bring the privacy tent for showering. I struggled a bit in August camping at Mora Campground which does not have a shower
- Breakfast, lunch, and dinner
- Camp kitchen (for cooking dinner)
- Croc shoes
- Park for the evening temperature. It may be in 70s during the day, but I need to be outside in the evening low of 40s.

## Hiking/Camping
- Bring hiking poles. I may regret not brining it (e.g. Mt. Storm King)
- Bring rain pants. I regret not bring it in Banff trip and bought an umbrella
