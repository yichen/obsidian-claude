This came from an idea when I discovered OpenClaw when I can chat with it from a chat program, and I thought about creating dedicated bots just for my kids, and the bot is individually trained to deal with specific kid.

For example, Ruby has Dyslexia, so besides chatting, I hope the app can be helpful in helping her learn to read. 

This redminds me of some features from tobeystutor.com, which I signed up for a month for the kids because they both have ADHD and Ruby has Dyslexia. 

I have been talking to my kids that I want to create a DaddyBot, so when they are with their mother (we are divorced), they can chat with me using the iPad app. 
I will have my backend to custom the bot's behavior, similar to the way I can provide the system prompt for an AI. 

I wonder if I can build a service for other parents, to allow their kids chat with a bot that are similar to them:
- The parents can use AI to record and train the voice, so the app can actually generate the parent's voice
- The parents can provide some preference on how they want the "daddybot", "mommybot" to behave, similar to the SOUL.md of OpenClaw.
- The parents can also provide instructions on how they want to direct the conversation with their kids. For example, they can be supportive (mental health), mentoring (teaching math) etc.

In a sense, it is similar to tobeystutor.com, but more customization for parents. The parents have more control, on what type of the subjects are OK to chat about.
