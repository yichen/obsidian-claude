# Trademark Collision Review: Domain Name Candidates for Personal Finance App

**Date**: 2026-03-17
**Analyst**: Claude (Opus)
**Product**: AI-powered local-first personal finance desktop app under Bamboo Leap (bambooleap.com)
**Relevant International Classes**: Class 9 (downloadable software), Class 36 (financial services/analysis), Class 42 (SaaS/computer software)
**Methodology**: Web research across USPTO trademark databases, company websites, Crunchbase, TechCrunch, app stores, domain registrations. 20+ web searches and 8 page fetches conducted.

---

## TL;DR

Every candidate name except "keepgrain" is already in active use by at least one well-funded company operating in software or financial services -- the exact classes where your product would file trademarks. "Trove" is the most dangerous: at least 6 active companies use it, including Trove Finance (a 400K-user investing app at trovefinance.com, Class 36 overlap), Trove Recommerce ($157M raised, trove.com), Trove Family Office (trove.net, UHNW financial services), and Microsoft holds a Class 9 TROVE trademark for downloadable software. "Fathom" is nearly as bad: 4 distinct companies already use it in software (Fathom AI notetaker at fathom.video/$30M raised, Fathom Analytics at usefathom.com, Fathom HQ financial software acquired by The Access Group, and Fathom.io enterprise intelligence). "Grain" has 3 active users in tech/fintech (grain.com AI notetaker, Grain Finance FX hedging with $50M+ raised, Grain Technology credit app with 470K users). The only two candidates without direct naming conflicts -- keeptrove.com and keepgrain.com -- still carry the core "Trove" and "Grain" marks, which means they inherit diluted risk from the parent names. Of all five candidates, **keepgrain.com is the safest** (no existing company uses "KeepGrain," and "Grain" conflicts are in different product categories), while **trove.app and fathom.app should be avoided entirely**.

## Candidate 1: trove.app

### The "Trove" Name is a Minefield with 6+ Active Claimants

The word "Trove" is one of the most overloaded brand names in technology and finance. A search reveals at least 6 distinct, active companies using this exact name:

| Company | Domain | What They Do | Funding | Class Overlap | Conflict Severity |
|---------|--------|-------------|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Trove Finance | trovefinance.com | Stock investing app (Nigeria, 400K+ users) | $120K seed | **Class 36 (financial services)** + Class 9 (mobile app) | **CRITICAL** |
| Trove Recommerce | trove.com | Branded resale platform (Patagonia, Levi's) | $157M total (Series E) | Class 9 (e-commerce software), Class 42 | High |
| Trove Family Office | trove.net | UHNW family office, financial admin | Unknown (PE-scale) | **Class 36 (financial management)** | **CRITICAL** |
| Microsoft TROVE | N/A | Trademark for downloadable software [A1] | $3T market cap | **Class 9 (downloadable software)** | **CRITICAL** |
| Foundry Payments TROVE | N/A | Trademark for mobile payment app [A2] | Unknown | **Class 36 (payment transactions)** + Class 9 | High |
| Trove Recommerce TROVE | N/A | Trademark for payment gateway + inventory software [A3] | $157M | Class 9 (software) | Medium |
| Trove, Inc. | N/A | AI relationship-building SaaS (Ann Arbor, MI) | Unknown | Class 42 (SaaS) | Medium |

**Trove Finance** is the single worst conflict. They are a financial services app with 400K+ users, SEC-registered partners, and active trademark use in the exact same product category -- personal finance software. Even though they primarily operate in Nigeria, their iOS/Android apps are listed on the US App Store and Google Play. They have clear prior use of "Trove" in Class 36 (financial services) and Class 9 (downloadable finance app).

**Trove Family Office** (trove.net) provides financial management, expense tracking, and bill payment services for UHNW families -- overlapping directly with the proposed product's Class 36 coverage. They operate from Boston, Stamford, NYC, and Salt Lake City.

**Microsoft's TROVE trademark** (Serial #90304112) covers Class 9 downloadable software. Microsoft has functionally unlimited legal resources to enforce trademarks.

**Domain situation**: trove.com is owned by Trove Recommerce ($157M company). The .app TLD does not provide meaningful trademark protection if the .com owner or other prior users assert priority.

**Collision Risk**: **HIGH**
**Specific Conflicts**: Trove Finance (direct financial app overlap), Trove Family Office (financial management), Microsoft (Class 9 software), Foundry Payments (mobile payment app)
**Legal Risk**: Near-certain cease-and-desist from at least one party. Trove Finance would have the strongest claim given identical product category.
**Recommendation**: **AVOID**

## Candidate 2: keeptrove.com

### A Prefix Reduces But Does Not Eliminate Trove's Conflicts

No company currently uses "KeepTrove" as a brand name. The domain appears unregistered (no results found for any existing business at keeptrove.com). This is the key advantage -- you would have first use of the compound mark.

However, "KeepTrove" contains the dominant element "Trove," which is already heavily used in financial services (see Candidate 1 analysis above). Under USPTO likelihood-of-confusion analysis, the addition of a generic/descriptive prefix like "Keep" provides only moderate differentiation. The test considers whether a consumer encountering "KeepTrove" in a finance app context would associate it with any of the existing "Trove" marks.

**Mitigating factors**:
- "KeepTrove" as a compound word has no existing usage -- you would be the first user
- The prefix "Keep" changes the semantic meaning (from "treasure find" to "preserve your treasure")
- The most dangerous conflict (Trove Finance) operates primarily in Nigeria, weakening their US trademark claim
- Microsoft's TROVE mark covers generic "downloadable software," not specifically finance

**Aggravating factors**:
- Trove Family Office (trove.net) provides financial management in the US -- direct geographic and class overlap
- If Trove Finance expands to the US market (their US App Store presence suggests intent), they have prior use
- Any marketing for "KeepTrove" will surface Trove Finance, Trove Recommerce, and Trove Family Office in search results, creating brand confusion

**Collision Risk**: **MEDIUM**
**Specific Conflicts**: Inherited from "Trove" mark -- primarily Trove Finance and Trove Family Office
**Legal Risk**: Lower than trove.app, but non-trivial. A C&D is possible but less likely since you have a distinct compound mark.
**Recommendation**: **PROCEED WITH CAUTION** -- get a formal trademark search and clearance opinion from an IP attorney before building the brand

## Candidate 3: fathom.app

### 4 Active Software Companies Named Fathom, Including a $30M AI Startup

"Fathom" is aggressively used across the software industry:

| Company | Domain | What They Do | Funding | Class Overlap | Conflict Severity |
|---------|--------|-------------|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Fathom Video (AI Notetaker) | fathom.video / fathom.ai | AI meeting recorder and notetaker | $30.2M (Series A, Sep 2024) | **Class 9 (downloadable software)** + Class 42 (SaaS) | **CRITICAL** |
| Fathom Analytics | usefathom.com | Privacy-first website analytics | Bootstrapped (Conva Ventures Inc., Canada) | Class 42 (SaaS), Class 9 | High |
| Fathom HQ (The Access Group) | fathomhq.com | Financial reporting & forecasting software | Acquired by The Access Group (UK PE firm) | **Class 36 (financial analysis)** + Class 42 + Class 9 | **CRITICAL** |
| Fathom.io | fathom.io | Enterprise intelligence OS | Unknown | Class 42 (data processing) | Medium |
| AFT Fathom | aft.com/products/fathom | Fluid dynamics simulation software | Established engineering company | Class 9 (software) | Low |

**Fathom HQ** is the most dangerous conflict. They are literally a financial software company -- "reporting, forecasting, and consolidation software" used by CFOs, business managers, and advisors. They have 7,500 customers across 81 countries. They were acquired by The Access Group, a UK PE-backed firm with deep legal resources. Filing a "Fathom" trademark in Class 36 (financial services) would directly collide with their existing mark.

**Fathom Video** ($30.2M raised, 90x revenue growth) operates at fathom.ai and fathom.video. They have explicit trademark protections in their ToS. While they are not in finance, they are in Class 9 (downloadable software) and Class 42 (SaaS) -- two of the three classes you need.

**Fathom Analytics** (Conva Ventures Inc., Victoria BC) explicitly states in their Terms of Service that "attempts to take unfair advantage of their product name, 'Fathom' or 'Fathom Analytics,' are in violation of their terms." They previously filed a FATHOM ANALYTICS trademark (Serial #88340464) which was abandoned, but they actively enforce the common-law mark.

**Domain situation**: fathom.app returned a connection refused error when fetched -- it may be parked, reserved, or not resolving. Regardless, fathom.com redirects/relates to multiple active Fathom companies. The .app domain provides no trademark insulation.

**Note on abandoned USPTO filings**: Two FATHOM trademark applications in the analytics/software space were abandoned (FATHOM RESEARCH+ANALYTICS, Serial #85520486; FATHOM ANALYTICS, Serial #88340464). However, abandonment of a USPTO registration does NOT mean abandonment of common-law trademark rights. All four companies listed above have active common-law marks through continuous commercial use.

**Collision Risk**: **HIGH**
**Specific Conflicts**: Fathom HQ (direct financial software overlap), Fathom Video ($30M AI company, Class 9/42), Fathom Analytics (Class 42, aggressive ToS)
**Legal Risk**: Very high probability of C&D from Fathom HQ (Access Group) given identical financial software category. Fathom Video also has resources and motivation to protect their mark.
**Recommendation**: **AVOID**

## Candidate 4: keepgrain.com

### The Safest Candidate -- "Grain" Conflicts Are in Different Categories

No company uses "KeepGrain" as a brand name. The domain appears unregistered. You would have clear first use of this compound mark.

The base word "Grain" has several active users in tech/fintech:

| Company | Domain | What They Do | Funding | Class Overlap | Conflict Severity |
|---------|--------|-------------|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Grain (AI Notetaker) | grain.com | AI meeting recording tool (83 employees, SF) | ~$10M+ (est.) | Class 42 (SaaS), Class 9 | Low-Medium |
| Grain Finance (FX Hedging) | grainfinance.com | Embedded FX hedging for platforms (Israel) | $50M+ ($33M Series A + $18M seed) | Class 36 (financial services) -- but B2B FX, not personal finance | Medium |
| Grain Technology (Credit App) | grain.credit | Consumer credit "crebit" card (Oakland, 470K users) | ~$180K | Class 36 (credit services), Class 9 | Medium |

**Key distinction**: Unlike "Trove" and "Fathom," none of the "Grain" companies operate in personal finance software for consumers. Grain Finance does FX hedging for B2B platforms. Grain Technology pivoted away from consumer credit to B2B products (Velex and Atlas) in 2023. Grain (AI Notetaker) does meeting recording. None competes in the "personal finance desktop app" space.

**The compound mark "KeepGrain" provides strong differentiation**:
- "Keep" + "Grain" creates a distinct semantic unit (preserve your money/savings)
- No existing company uses this compound
- The "Grain" companies operate in sufficiently different product categories that likelihood-of-confusion is low
- Grain Technology's pivot away from consumer products further reduces conflict

**Remaining risks**:
- Grain Finance (grainfinance.com, $50M+ raised) could argue confusion if you market "KeepGrain" as financial software, but their product (B2B embedded FX) is very different from personal finance tracking
- The word "grain" in a finance context has a natural meaning ("keep your grain" = keep your money), which supports distinctiveness
- SEO competition: searching "KeepGrain" will not surface grain.com or grainfinance.com (different compound)

**Collision Risk**: **LOW**
**Specific Conflicts**: Weak/distant -- Grain Finance (B2B FX, different category), Grain Technology (pivoted away from consumer)
**Legal Risk**: Low probability of C&D. No existing company has a strong basis to challenge "KeepGrain" in Class 36 personal finance software.
**Recommendation**: **SAFE** -- strongest candidate of the five. Still recommend a formal trademark search before filing.

## Candidate 5: ownfathom.com

### The Prefix Helps, But Fathom HQ's Financial Software Overlap Is Too Direct

No company uses "OwnFathom" as a brand name. The domain appears unregistered.

However, "OwnFathom" inherits the same severe conflicts as fathom.app (see Candidate 3), with one difference: the "Own" prefix provides slightly more differentiation than a bare "Fathom" mark.

**The problem remains Fathom HQ**: They are a financial reporting and forecasting software company with 7,500 customers, acquired by a well-resourced PE firm. "OwnFathom" used for a personal finance app would likely trigger a likelihood-of-confusion challenge from The Access Group. The semantic link is too direct -- "own your financial fathom/insight" reads as a derivative of "Fathom" financial software.

**Compared to keeptrove.com**: The "Fathom" conflicts are worse because Fathom HQ is a direct financial software competitor (7,500 customers, PE-backed), whereas "Trove" in finance is split across a Nigerian investing app and a US family office. Fathom HQ has clearer standing to challenge.

**Mitigating factors**:
- "OwnFathom" is a compound not used by anyone
- Fathom Video (fathom.ai) is in meeting recording, not finance
- Fathom Analytics is in web analytics, not finance

**Aggravating factors**:
- Fathom HQ is literally financial software with US customers
- The Access Group (acquirer) is a large UK PE firm with legal resources
- 4 different Fathom companies in software creates a "crowded field" that cuts both ways -- it may make it harder to register but also harder for any one company to claim exclusive rights

**Collision Risk**: **MEDIUM-HIGH**
**Specific Conflicts**: Fathom HQ (direct financial software overlap, PE-backed), Fathom Video ($30M, Class 9/42)
**Legal Risk**: Moderate-to-high. Fathom HQ's Access Group parent has resources and motivation to enforce in the financial software category.
**Recommendation**: **PROCEED WITH CAUTION** -- but inferior to keepgrain.com and keeptrove.com

## Recommended Next Steps

1. **If choosing keepgrain.com**: Run a formal USPTO TESS search for "GRAIN" and "KEEPGRAIN" in Classes 9, 36, and 42 before filing. The $250-$350 filing fee is trivial compared to the cost of a rebrand. Consider also registering keepgrain.app for defensive purposes.

2. **If choosing keeptrove.com**: Get a formal clearance opinion from a trademark attorney (budget $500-$1,500). The "Trove" mark's density in financial services makes this a judgment call that warrants professional review.

3. **Avoid trove.app and fathom.app entirely**: The collision density is too high. Building a brand on either name is planting a lawsuit.

4. **Consider entirely different names**: Given that 3 of 5 candidates have issues, it may be worth brainstorming names that are not common English dictionary words. Compound neologisms (like "bambooleap") have far lower trademark collision risk than single dictionary words.
