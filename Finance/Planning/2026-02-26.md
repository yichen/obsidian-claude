# 2026 401(k) Catch-Up Optimization Plan

##TL;DR

- Adjustment made to ServiceTitan 401k, increased pre-tax from 6% to 9%, and reduced post-tax from 15% to zero.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Objective

Prioritize full utilization of age 50+ elective deferral (including
catch-up) while preserving employer match and maintaining flexibility
for mega backdoor after-tax contributions later in the year.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. IRS Contribution Limits (Age 50+)

-   Base elective deferral limit: \$23,000\
-   Catch-up contribution: \$7,500\
-   Total elective deferral allowed (Pre-tax + Roth combined across all
    employers):

**\$30,500**

Important: - This limit is shared across all employers. - After-tax
contributions do NOT count toward this limit. - After-tax contributions
count toward the \$69,000 415(c) per-employer cap.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Total baseline pre-tax ≈ \$20,880\
→ Not fully utilizing \$30,500 elective deferral limit\

-   Below \$30,500 (no overshoot risk)

-   Leaves \~\$2,120 buffer for safety

Marginal tax rate ≈ 37%

Every additional \$1 of pre-tax deferral saves: \$0.37 in federal tax
immediately

Unused elective deferral space costs: 37 cents per dollar in avoidable
tax.

Therefore: Maximizing pre-tax (including catch-up) takes priority over
early-year mega backdoor contributions.
