**What was learned**:
- OFW's moat is institutional, not financial: 25 years of Judicial Education Coordinators, CLE credits, AFCC Diamond sponsorship, court order template language. No kickbacks to lawyers.
- OFW practitioner dashboard shows everything (all messages, calendar, expenses, login history with IP) — requires parent consent to connect. Yi has never connected his lawyer.
- Final parenting plan Section o mandates OFW for communication, but Section n allows mutual written agreements to deviate from any provision — switching to a cheaper app is legally safe if both agree in writing.
- TalkingParents bootstrapped to $10M+ ARR over 14 years — proves bootstrap path exists but it's slow.
- Cooperative (non-court-ordered) co-parents are a distinct, underserved segment. Every incumbent built for the courtroom; cooperative parents need different messaging ("stay organized together" not "document everything").
- BestInterest (communication-focused) and proposed app (schedule+payments) would be complementary, not competitive.
- Market expansion beyond coparenting: separated couples with spousal support but no kids can also use payments feature.
- .app domains cost ~$11-20/year depending on registrar. Cloudflare and Porkbun are cheapest.

**What was decided**:
- Coparenting app is a lifestyle business target ($500K-$2M ARR), NOT venture-scale
- Focus on schedule + payments only — don't compete with BestInterest on communication
- Target cooperative non-court-ordered parents via SEO, mediator partnerships, free tier
- No practitioner dashboard in Year 1 — not targeting court-ordered users
- 90-day $0 validation experiments before committing to full build
- Domain candidates: fairbridge.app (top pick), twoaccord.com, twofair.com, parentpact.app
