**What was learned**:
- .app domains cost ~$11-20/yr (Cloudflare cheapest at ~$11). Google owns the .app TLD, enforces HTTPS.
- Two-word .com domains are almost impossible to find available — need a suffix (labs, tech) or three words
- "Jade Garden" = Chinese restaurant connotation. "Jade Bridge" = soy sauce brand (UK). "Jade Leap" feels fresh and avoids clichés.
- "Split" in a brand name carries negative divorce connotation — avoid for coparenting products
- Names like "loom," "forge," "earnest" feel medieval/artisan — user prefers modern, clean names
- The pattern [adjective][noun]labs.com has good availability: jadeleaplabs.com, longleaplabs.com, boldshiftlabs.com all available
- Chinese-inspired naming works if the Chinese element (jade, bamboo, crane) is paired with a modern/action word (leap, path, bridge) rather than traditional (garden, palace, gate)
- User previously owned TinyTimeTable.com (TTT acronym) and ColorfulMemory.com — prefers evocative two-word names
