### Impact Statement:

*Reflect how the impairment impacts the student's access to education to help teachers understand what they might observe in the classroom, how that relates to the impairment(s), and strategies that could reduce the impact/help the student access their education*

Ruby is a determined, bright student who is impacted by her ADHD- Combined Type, Sensory Processing Issues, Specific Learning Disorder in Reading Comprehension (Dyslexia), and Generalized Anxiety Disorder. Ruby has sensory sensitivities, often becoming unfocused or overstimulated when she is in a loud environment. Ruby is still working to develop her confidence and independence at school, specifically related to literacy. When Ruby is instructed to start an assignment, she shows learned helplessness in which she immediately asks her teacher for help before trying on her own. Ruby often shares that she does not know how to do the task, or hesitates to initiate the task at all. Though Ruby continues to develop literacy skills, she has a hard time applying them independently or will make mistakes due to rushing in her reading and writing. Ruby's dyslexia and difficulties with phonics make spelling challenging for her, and she struggles to recall how to spell words when she is feeling anxious or pressured. Ruby is motivated to use the resources provided to her appropriately to help when she gets stuck on spelling a word. It is challenging for Ruby to focus her attention during writing, leading to challenges in organizing her thoughts and getting them down on paper. Ruby engages in negative self-talk during writing times, becoming discouraged when tasks feel challenging. Due to Ruby's dyslexia, reading comprehension, fluency, and decoding makes it hard for her to access grade-level texts.

### Accommodations necessary to access education:

1. Provide access to noise-cancelling headphones

2. Allow Ruby to start assignments in a small group setting to help initiate the task. Support may include breaking down directions or chunking the assignment. Once Ruby understands the assignment, she should work independently.

3. Provide access to a picture dictionary to support Ruby's spelling

4. Provide access to graphic organizers to assist in organization of writing tasks

5. Provide access to audiobook programs to assist with reading (e.g., Learning Ally or other approved platforms).
