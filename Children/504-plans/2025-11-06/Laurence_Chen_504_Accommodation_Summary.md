### Impact Statement:

*Reflect how the impairment impacts the student's access to education to help teachers understand what they might observe in the classroom, how that relates to the impairment(s), and strategies that could reduce the impact/help the student access their education*

Laurence is an enthusiastic, hardworking student who is impacted by his ADHD- Combined Type, Sensory Processing Issues, Specific Learning Disorder in Reading Comprehension (Dyslexia), and Generalized Anxiety Disorder. Laurence is still working to develop his confidence and tends to shut down rather than discussing his emotions or challenges with others. Laurence continues to strengthen his confidence through creating and sharing his creations with others. Laurence is able to learn and apply new skills quickly and does best when he has flexibility and choice in how he demonstrates his learning. Because of his disabilities, executive functioning is challenging for Laurence. He can have a hard time managing multiple steps, doing steps in the correct order, initiating tasks, and knowing what to do once his tasks are completed. Laurence needs additional prompts and reminders in order to complete routines and assignments independently. It can be challenging for Laurence to retain multistep directions that are delivered auditorily. Laurence can be impulsive and sometimes struggles to regulate his emotions. He occasionally becomes physical with other students when he is overstimulated. Laurence needs nonverbal reminders to stay focused and on-task, in which his teachers can redirect him to expected behaviors. Laurence has made significant improvements with his impulsivity through practicing expected behavior by being close to the teacher during instruction times and while in line.

### Accommodations necessary to access education:

1. Provide opportunities for personalized recognition when Laurence meets his goals (ex: tally system for being on task, glow/grow notes, earning the chance to show his skills and talents to the class)

2. Provide flexibility and choice in how Laurence expresses and demonstrates his learning (ex: utilizing technology such as Book Creator, Canva, etc.). Allow flexible seating during independent work times.

3. Provide visuals and checklists to support Laurence with independence during daily routines, independent work times, and tests.

4. Provide Laurence with preferential seating where he has a direct line of sight to the point of instruction and board. Provide preferential line placement where Laurence is close to his teacher as needed.
