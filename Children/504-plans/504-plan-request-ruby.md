## Requested 504 Accommodations

### 1. Assistive Technology
- Text-to-speech software for reading assignments
- Audiobooks for grade-level content (allows her to access curriculum while working on decoding)
- Speech-to-text for written assignments

### 2. Extended Time & Pacing
- 1.5x extended time on reading comprehension tasks and all assessments
- Untimed tests when appropriate
- Extra time for multi-step assignments

### 3. Reading Comprehension Support
- Pre-teaching vocabulary before new units
- Graphic organizers for story elements, sequencing, and inferences
- Read-alouds with oral comprehension checks (to assess understanding separate from decoding)
- Access to highlighted/annotated texts

### 4. ADHD & Processing Support
- Preferential seating (front of class, minimal distractions)
- Movement breaks every 20-30 minutes
- Fidget tools during instruction
- Chunked assignments with written/visual checklists
- Reduced visual clutter in workspace
- Quiet testing space

### 5. Anxiety Support
- Regular check-ins with counselor or trusted adult
- Permission to take breaks when overwhelmed
- Advance notice of schedule changes
- Option to respond privately rather than publicly in class

### 6. Alternative Assessment
- Oral responses as alternative to written when appropriate
- Portfolio-based assessment to demonstrate growth over time

### 7. Specialized Intervention
- Daily specialized reading intervention using evidence-based dyslexia programs (Orton-Gillingham or Wilson Reading System)
- Small group or 1:1 reading instruction focusing on comprehension strategies
- Monthly progress monitoring via iReady or similar assessment

### 8. Dual Assessment Request
- Separate evaluation of listening comprehension vs. reading comprehension to document the gap between her strong oral comprehension and reading comprehension (this will support the assistive technology accommodations)

## Rationale

The neuropsych evaluation and iReady data demonstrate that Ruby has strong underlying comprehension abilities (evidenced by her conversational understanding and strong math performance), but her **dyslexia is blocking her access to text**. The significant drop in reading percentiles—despite absolute score improvement—shows she is falling further behind peers as text complexity increases.

**Ruby needs accommodations that separate decoding from comprehension** so she can access grade-level content while receiving intensive, evidence-based dyslexia intervention. Without these supports, she risks falling significantly further behind academically.
