# Endeavour Elementary Guidance Team

## What is Guidance Team?

Guidance Team is a group of school staff and a student's family who come together to discuss educational interventions. The goal of the team meeting is to collaborate and try to solve problems that are impacting a student's educational success.

### We will discuss:

- Your child's academic and social strengths
- Developmental and background information about your child
- Concerns that you and/or your child's teacher have
- Strategies that have been attempted
- Development of an action plan to help meet your child's needs

Strategies may include interventions or accommodations available in the school, suggestions and/or particular instructional strategies, community resources, or gathering additional information.

As parents, you provide an important perspective on your child's strengths and individual needs. We may ask you about your child's development.

---

## Meeting Information

Our Guidance Team meets weekly for approximately 45-60 minutes per student. Please check in at the office and a member of our team will invite you into the conference room. We strive to begin and end on time.

If you have any questions about the guidance team process, please feel free to contact your child's teacher or the school psychologist, Mark Geers, at geersm@issaquah.wednet.edu

If you are unable to attend guidance team please reach out to re-schedule a new time, or meeting format (i.e. phone conference) that is amenable.

---

## Further Assessment

At times, our team may recommend further assessment to determine eligibility for 504 accommodation plan or special education services. If the team recommends an evaluation, the school counselor, Mikayla Rolfs; or school psychologist, Mark Geers, will be in contact about the next steps.
