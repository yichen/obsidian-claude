## Email Draft

Hi Mikayla,

Hope you're doing well! I wanted to follow up on your email from August where you mentioned you'd distributed Ruby and Laurence's 504 accommodations to their new teachers and asked if there was anything we'd like to discuss.

Now that we have their **September iReady results**, I'm seeing some concerning patterns, especially for Ruby, and I was hoping we could schedule a time to chat about whether a few additional accommodations might help.

### What's Happening with Ruby (My Bigger Concern)

Ruby's reading has taken a pretty significant hit this year, even though she's doing great in math:

- **Reading comprehension dropped from 82nd percentile → 63rd percentile** (19-point decline)
- **Literary text comprehension** went from being a **strength last year → "Needs Improvement"** now
- **Vocabulary and informational text** also went from strengths to below grade level
- The good news is she's **mastered all the foundational stuff** (phonics, high-frequency words)
- Her **math is still strong at 92nd percentile**, so we know her cognitive ability is there

The neuropsych evaluation from December confirmed she has dyslexia. While she's made good progress on foundational decoding skills (phonics, high-frequency words), her reading fluency isn't automatic yet. I think what we're seeing is that as the text gets more complex in 2nd grade, she's using so much mental energy on decoding that there's not enough left over for comprehension.

### What's Happening with Laurence

Laurence is doing better overall, but there are still some spots where he's struggling:

- **Informational text comprehension** went from a **strength → "Approaching Grade 2"** (his only below-grade area now)
- **Behavioral stuff continues** - his emotional regulation is still "Emerging" according to his June report card
- His **math is exceptional at 99th percentile**, so like Ruby, his cognitive ability is clearly there

The neuropsych also confirmed dyslexia for him, plus working memory challenges that create some barriers.

### What I'm Hoping We Can Try

The current accommodations have been helpful, but the September data shows we need to do more - especially for Ruby whose reading dropped 19 percentile points. I'd like to add some key supports that the neuropsych recommended, particularly text-to-speech and extended time, which are pretty standard for kids with dyslexia.

**For Both Kids:**
- **Text-to-speech software** for reading assignments (allows access to grade-level content while building reading fluency)
- **Audiobooks** for grade-level content - I recently learned that Bookshare is free for students with 504 plans (funded by the Dept of Education) and has a huge library. I know Clever has a small Learning Ally collection, but Bookshare might give them better access to grade-level texts
- **Extended time (1.5x)** on reading/writing tasks
- **Graphic organizers** for comprehension strategies (story elements, inferences, sequencing)

**Additional for Laurence:**
- **Visual supports** for working memory (written directions, checklists, etc.)
- **More structured check-ins** with the counselor for emotional regulation support - he's currently in weekly therapy, and coordinated school support would help reinforce the strategies he's learning

These are standard accommodations recommended by the neuropsych for dyslexia and ADHD. I'm happy to help with applications or paperwork for things like Bookshare if that would be helpful.

### Can We Meet?

Would you have 30-45 minutes sometime in the next couple weeks to talk this through? I'm flexible with timing and totally fine with Zoom if that's easier. I can bring the September iReady reports and anything else that would be helpful.

I really appreciate the 504 plans you put together back in the spring. I just want to make sure we're catching this reading decline early, especially for Ruby, before the gap gets any wider.

Let me know what works for you!
