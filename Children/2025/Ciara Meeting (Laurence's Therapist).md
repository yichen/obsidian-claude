## I. Impulse Control and Emotional Regulation

### 🧠 Core Issue
* Laurence exhibits strong emotions and **lack of impulse regulation** (common with ADHD).
* He has expressed frustration toward Ruby, including saying he wants to hurt her, followed by affirmations of love.
* **Focus:** Channel energy elsewhere, away from Ruby.

### 🧠 The Brain on Anger
* When angry, the **Prefrontal Cortex** (logical decision-making/emotion regulation center) turns off. 
* **Implication:** Do not attempt to talk to or reason with Laurence when he is angry. He will not hear you, and may even do the exact opposite of what you advise.

### 🛑 Post-Behavior Conversation Strategy (Regulate, Then Reflect)
The crucial shift is to move beyond just asking for an apology.

| Step | Action | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Regulate** | Wait until Laurence is completely **calm and regulated** (not angry or highly emotional). | Ensures his Prefrontal Cortex is engaged and he can actually process what you say. |
| **2. Reflect** | Ask reflective questions: **"Why did you do that?"** or **"What prompted you to do this?"** | Encourages him to pause, reflect on the impulse, and articulate his feelings/thoughts. When he says "I don't know," it confirms the action was impulsive. |
| **3. Alternatives** | Discuss replacement behaviors: **"What can you do instead?"** (e.g., hit a pillow, ask for a break). | Teaches him constructive ways to handle frustration and gives him an opportunity to choose a better outcome next time. |

## II. Token Economy (Behavior Management Plan)

### 🌟 Core Principle
**Reward good behavior instead of punishing bad behavior.** This reinforces positive actions and teaches him to seek attention through desired conduct.

* *Observation:* Laurence is highly motivated by rewards. He is testing the waters for attention; give attention to positive acts, not negative ones.

### 💰 How the System Works

1.  **Tokens for Good Behavior:** Give a token every time Laurence exhibits a desired behavior (e.g., using a calming strategy instead of hitting Ruby; completing homework/math practice).
2.  **Tokens are Never Taken Away:** Tokens are earned and deserved. If he misbehaves, he simply **does not earn a token** at that moment.
3.  **Rewards:** Laurence turns in accumulated tokens for desired rewards (he is very motivated by screen time/iPad).
    * **Set Boundaries:** Use the tokens to set boundaries (e.g., "You must earn X tokens to get 5 minutes more screen time").
    * **Reward Menu:** Develop a menu with daily, weekly, and large rewards (e.g., 1 token = 5 minutes screen time; saving up for an iPad).

### 🛠️ Implementation Tips
* **Consistency:** The goal is to keep this plan consistent between both households.
* **Start Small/Time Box:** Initially, you can choose a specific time window (e.g., 4:00 PM to 5:00 PM) where you actively look for and reward good behavior with tokens, rather than constantly trying to monitor all day.
* **Collaboration:** Talk to Laurence about the plan and let him help choose the rewards.
