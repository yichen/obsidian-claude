## Memory Assessment

### RCFT (Rey Complex Figure Test)

The RCFT provided an opportunity to objectively evaluate Ruby's metacognitive functions, such as planning, organizing, monitoring, and selecting, as well as grapho-motor output and visual perception.

**Test Description:**
Ruby was asked to visually process a novel, complex, and unusual geometric figure, encode the details, and retrieve the encoded information from memory to reproduce the image by hand. Metacognitive functions were assessed through the evaluation of three freehand drawing trials:
1. Copy
2. Immediate recall
3. Delayed recall

A 36-point scoring system based on organization and accuracy of placement criteria was used.

**Results:**

**Copy Trial:**
- Ruby approached this motor construction task in an organized, systematic manner
- **Significant spatial distortion and spatial displacement were apparent**

**Recall Trials:**
- **No visual retrieval** in either immediate or delayed time periods when asked to reproduce the geometric figure
- Difficulties with visual encoding when motor coordination was involved

**Recognition Trial:**
- Performance was **within the average range**
- Indicates more satisfactory visual encoding and retrieval when visuomotor coordination was not involved

**Clinical Interpretation:**
These difficulties with visual processing, motor coordination, and memory retrieval are consistent with characteristics often observed in individuals with neurodiversity, including:
- Attention deficit with sensory processing issues
- **Dyslexia**
