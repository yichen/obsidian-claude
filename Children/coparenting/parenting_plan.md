### 13. MOVING WITH THE CHILDREN (RELOCATION)

Both parents acknowledge that the mother is planning to move. So long as she does not move more than 15 miles from her current residence, the father agrees not to object. The children shall remain in their current schools unless otherwise agreed.

Anyone with majority or substantially equal residential time (at least 45 percent) who wants to move with the children must notify every other person who has court-ordered time with the children.

#### Move to a different school district

If the move is to a different school district, the relocating person must complete the form Notice of Intent to Move with Children (FL Relocate 701) and deliver it at least **60 days** before the intended move.

**Exceptions:**
- If the relocating person could not reasonably have known enough information to complete the form in time to give 60 days' notice, they must give notice within **5 days** after learning the information.
- If the relocating person is relocating to a domestic violence shelter or moving to avoid a clear, immediate, and unreasonable risk to health or safety, notice may be delayed **21 days**.
- If information is protected under a court order or the address confidentiality program, it may be withheld from the notice.
- A relocating person who believes that giving notice would put themself or a child at unreasonable risk of harm, may ask the court for permission to leave things out of the notice or to be allowed to move without giving notice.

#### Move within the same school district

If the move is within the same school district, the relocating person still has to let the other parent know. However, the notice does not have to be served personally or by mail with a return receipt. Notice to the other party can be made in any reasonable way. No specific form is required.

#### Right to object

A person who has court-ordered time with the children can object to a move to a different school district and/or to the relocating person's proposed Parenting Plan. An objection must be filed and served no later than **30 days** after the Notice of Intent to Move with Children was received.

#### Right to move

During the 30 days after the Notice was served, the relocating person may not move to a different school district with the children unless they have a court order allowing the move.

After the 30 days, if no Objection is filed, the relocating person may move with the children without getting a court order allowing the move.

After the 30 days, if an Objection has been filed, the relocating person may move with the children pending the final hearing on the Objection unless:
- The other party gets a court order saying the children cannot move, or
- The other party has scheduled a hearing to take place no more than 15 days after the date the Objection was served on the relocating person.

### 14. OTHER

#### a. Address/Telephone Numbers

Both parents shall be required to keep the other advised of their current residential and employment addresses, as well as their telephone numbers at all times. Both parents shall notify the other regarding any permanent changes to their work schedule. Neither parent shall telephone the other or address mail to the other at their place of employment except to discuss an emergency situation. Each parent will keep the other parent informed at all times of an emergency contact if that parent and/or the children will be out-of-town.

#### b. Telephone Access

When the children are not residing with a given parent, they shall be permitted reasonable telephone/video call and email access with that parent at reasonable times and for reasonable durations.

#### c. Mail Access

The parents and the children shall enjoy unrestricted mail and email access to each other.

#### d. Access to Records

Each parent shall have the right and responsibility to ensure that the children attend school and other scheduled activities while in that parent's care. Each parent shall have full and equal access to the education, daycare and health records of the children (except to the extent that a separate consent may need to be obtained for a child as provided by law). Both parents shall have equal and independent authority, as provided by statute, to confer with the school.

#### e. Medical Directive

Both parents shall follow the medical directives of the children's health care providers and physicians including giving prescribed medication. Each parent shall notify the other parent promptly of any medication that has been prescribed for the children.

#### f. Attending Activities

The parents shall be responsible for keeping themselves advised of athletic, academic and social events in which the children participate. Both parents may participate in school and extracurricular activities for the children regardless of whether the activity falls during their residential time. Both parents shall be responsible for transporting the children to all agreed upon activities during their residential time PROVIDED THAT each parent may elect to skip up to one weekend's activit(ies) a quarter to attend other planned activities or trips with the children.

#### g. Children's Involvement

Neither parent shall ask the children to make decisions or requests involving the residential schedule. Neither parent shall encourage the children to change their primary residence or encourage the children to believe it is their choice to do so. Neither parent shall discuss changes to the residential schedule which have not been agreed to by both parents in advance. Neither parent shall advise the children of the status of child support payments or other legal matters regarding the parent's relationship. Neither parent shall use the children, directly or indirectly, to gather information about the other parent or to take verbal messages to the other parent.

#### h. Homework

Each parent shall ensure the children complete all homework they are required to complete during that parent's residential time. Each parent is responsible for keeping themselves apprised of the children's homework assignments.

#### i. Derogatory Comments

Neither parent shall make derogatory comments about the other parent or allow anyone else to do so in the children's presence. Neither parent shall allow or encourage the children to make derogatory comments about the other parent.

#### j. Travel Notification

Each parent shall inform the other parent when that parent plans to be away from their residence with the children overnight. The information to be provided shall include duration of the period, the destination(s) and destination telephone number(s) including cell phone numbers.

#### k. International Travel

Neither parent may leave the United States (excluding Canada) with the children without written notice to the other parent and agreement (e-mail or OFW notice shall suffice and agreement shall not be unreasonably withheld). Any disputes about whether a parent may travel internationally with the children shall be submitted to dispute resolution. If an emergency, the party may seek an ex parte order with notice to the other parent. 

Travel with the children to any country that is not a signatory to The Hague Convention on the Civil Aspects of International Child Abduction with a US treaty is prohibited unless the non-traveling parents signs a written consent (authorization by e-mail is insufficient) containing the detailed itinerary of the trip and agreeing that the children will be returned on a specified date.

If necessary for travel, the parents shall cooperate in obtaining the children's passports and keeping the passports valid. **Yi Chen shall hold the children's passports.** If visas or other documentation are required for foreign travel, the parents shall cooperate in good faith to obtain such visas and provide such documentation. 

The United States is the habitual residence of the children and a refusal to return the children to the United States by either parent shall be conclusively deemed wrongful under the Convention. 

When the father travels to China he shall return to the United States so that the children have **2 days (48 hours) to reacclimate** to the pacific time zone before being returned to the mother and commencing school.

#### l. Routine of Children

Both parents shall try to maintain the children's regular routine and bedtime schedule during their residential time with them. Both parents shall facilitate this by providing timely information and updates about the children's schedule and routines.

#### m. Emergency Notification

Each parent shall notify the other promptly but in any event **no later than 24 hours** of receipt of extraordinary information regarding the children, such as emergency medical care, major school discipline, unusual or unexplained absence from the home, or contact with police or other legal authority.

#### n. Agreements and Waiver

The parents are free at any time to make agreements between the two of them that are different than the residential and nonresidential provisions of this parenting plan. Agreements shall be in writing (agreements documented by email are sufficient). An agreement to act differently concerning a provision on one or more occasions does not result in a waiver of that provision in the future, nor does it result in a waiver of any other provisions or create adequate cause for future modification.

#### o. Parental Communication

The parents shall communicate via **Our Family Wizard**, except in the case of an emergency in which case text or phone calls may be used.

#### p. Corporal Punishment

No corporal punishment may be used with the children. The children may not be locked in a room as a form of punishment.

#### q. Therapy

The parents shall each continue in individual therapy and they must provide copies of both the Parental Evaluation completed by Dr. English, and this Final Parenting Plan to their providers.
