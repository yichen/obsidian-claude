## 🧠 1. Support Core Learning Habits  
**Goal:** Strengthen Ruby’s independence, confidence, and reading foundations.

- ✅ Encourage her to **try first before asking for help** — praise effort, not just correctness (reduces “learned helplessness”).  
- ✅ Give **short, specific directions** and have her repeat them back.  
- ✅ Offer **quiet work time** — use noise-canceling headphones if she’s easily overstimulated.  
- ✅ Use **visual checklists** for routines (homework, packing backpack, bedtime).

---

## 📖 2. Reading & Dyslexia Support  
**Goal:** Reinforce decoding, comprehension, and confidence.

- ✅ Practice daily reading (~20 min): alternate between **listening and reading along** (Learning Ally, Audible, or read aloud).  
- ✅ Focus on **decoding and phonics** — gently sound out unfamiliar words together.  
- ✅ Use **picture dictionaries** or visual word walls to support spelling and sight-word recall.  
- ✅ Choose **grade-appropriate audiobooks** to build comprehension while keeping reading enjoyable.  
- ✅ After reading or listening, ask her to **retell the story** or **draw a scene** to strengthen comprehension.

---

## 🧮 3. Math & Attention Focus  
**Goal:** Leverage her math strengths while maintaining attention and structure.

- ✅ Keep math practice short and visual — games, manipulatives, or whiteboard problem-solving.  
- ✅ Reinforce **real-world math** (cooking, counting change, measuring time).  
- ✅ Allow **breaks every 10–15 minutes** of focused work to sustain attention.

---

## 💬 4. Emotional & Anxiety Regulation  
**Goal:** Build resilience and positive self-talk.

- ✅ Use **positive affirmations** daily (“I can try first,” “I’m a good reader when I slow down”).  
- ✅ Help her **name feelings** (“I feel frustrated because…”) and use calm-down tools (breathing, art, short walk).  
- ✅ Avoid high-pressure or timed tasks — praise persistence over speed.  
- ✅ Keep transitions predictable; **give advance notice** before changes.

---

## 🎨 5. Confidence & Motivation  
**Goal:** Nurture Ruby’s creative and determined personality.

- ✅ Set **small, achievable goals** and celebrate successes (“You read that paragraph all by yourself!”).  
- ✅ Integrate her interests — art, storytelling, gymnastics — into learning.  
- ✅ Encourage **choice and ownership** (“Do you want to read this or that book first?”).  
- ✅ Use **recognition systems** (stickers, points, “I did it!” chart) to reinforce effort and independence.
