## 5. Decision-Making

When the children are with you, you are responsible for them. You can make **day-to-day decisions** for the children when the children are with you, including decisions about safety and emergency healthcare.

**Major decisions** must be made as follows:

### a. Who can make major decisions about the children?

| Type of Major Decision | Decision Authority |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| **School / Educational** | Joint (parents make these decisions together) |
| **Healthcare** (not emergency) including therapy/counseling | Joint (parents make these decisions together) |
| **Extracurricular activities** including camps that require a financial or time contribution from both parents | Joint (parents make these decisions together) |

#### Additional Provisions:

- The children can participate in **at least two activities every quarter**.
- Consent to activities for the children **cannot be unreasonably withheld**.
- If a child needs support in an academic area based on whether a child is **at grade level**, the parents agree to obtain **tutoring** for the child in that area.
- Although healthcare is a joint decision, the parents agree that the **mother may make routine well-child check-ups and biannual dental cleanings** during her residential time. This authority does not extend to non-routine appointments or evaluations.
  - The mother will give the other parent notice of the appointments being made as soon as the appointments are scheduled.
  - Both parents may attend any appointments if they so desire.
  - If either parent is unable to attend, the attending parent shall provide the other parent with information gained during the appointment.

### Process for Decision Making

1. The parent who proposes a decision will notify the other parent **in writing** of the nature of the proposed decision including the description, cost, schedule, time period, etc.
2. As much notice as possible will be given, but **not less than 72 hours** except in the case of an emergency.
3. The responding parent will agree, disagree, or propose an alternative to the proposed action plan, **in writing, within 72 hours** unless the parties agree to a longer response time in writing.
   - **A parent may request an extension of an additional 48 hours to respond.**
4. Consent to a proposal will **not be unreasonably withheld**.
5. If **no timely objection or response is made**, the parent who is requesting the decision may implement his/her proposed action plan.
6. If the parents **cannot reach an agreement**, either parent may submit the issue to the **Dispute Resolution process** in §6 of the Parenting Plan.
7. **Pending an agreement or Dispute Resolution decision**, either parent may take action (i.e., such as making an appointment with a provider) but may not commit the child until such time as the issue has been resolved.
   - That parent will be solely responsible for any **non-refundable deposit or cancellation fee** incurred if the arbitrator does not allow the proposal.

### b. Reasons for limits on major decision-making, if any:

**There are no reasons to limit major decision-making.**

## 13. Moving with the Children (Relocation)

### Current Moving Plan:

Both parents acknowledge that the **mother is planning to move**. So long as she does not move **more than 15 miles from her current residence**, the father agrees not to object.

The children shall **remain in their current schools** unless otherwise agreed.

### General Relocation Rules:

Anyone with majority or substantially equal residential time (at least 45 percent) who wants to move with the children must notify every other person who has court-ordered time with the children.

#### Move to a Different School District

If the move is to a different school district, the relocating person must complete the form **Notice of Intent to Move with Children (FL Relocate 701)** and deliver it **at least 60 days before** the intended move.

**Exceptions:**

- If the relocating person could not reasonably have known enough information to complete the form in time to give 60 days' notice, they must give notice **within 5 days** after learning the information.
- If the relocating person is relocating to a **domestic violence shelter** or moving to avoid a clear, immediate, and unreasonable risk to health or safety, notice may be delayed **21 days**.
- If information is protected under a court order or the **address confidentiality program**, it may be withheld from the notice.
- A relocating person who believes that giving notice would put themself or a child at unreasonable risk of harm, may ask the court for permission to leave things out of the notice or to be allowed to move without giving notice. Use form **Motion to Limit Notice of Intent to Move with Children (Ex Parte) (FL Relocate 702)**.

The Notice of Intent to Move with Children can be delivered by having someone personally serve the other party or by any form of mail that requires a return receipt.

If the relocating person wants to change the Parenting Plan because of the move, they must deliver a **proposed Parenting Plan** together with the Notice.

#### Move Within the Same School District

If the move is within the same school district, the relocating person still has to let the other parent know. However, the notice does not have to be served personally or by mail with a return receipt. Notice to the other party can be made in any reasonable way. No specific form is required.

### Warning

A relocating person who does not give the required notice may be found in **contempt of court**. If that happens, the court can impose sanctions. Sanctions can include:
- Requiring the relocating person to bring the children back if the move has already happened
- Ordering the relocating person to pay the other side's costs and lawyer's fees

### Right to Object

A person who has court-ordered time with the children can object to a move to a different school district and/or to the relocating person's proposed Parenting Plan.

If the move is within the same school district, the other party doesn't have the right to object to the move, but they may ask to change the Parenting Plan if there are adequate reasons under the modification law (RCW 26.09.260).

An objection is made by filing the **Objection about Moving with Children and Petition about Changing a Parenting/Custody Order (Relocation) (form FL Relocate 721)**.

- File your Objection with the court and serve a copy on the relocating person and anyone else who has court-ordered time with the children.
- Service of the Objection must be by personal service or by mailing a copy to each person by any form of mail that requires a return receipt.
- The Objection must be filed and served **no later than 30 days** after the Notice of Intent to Move with Children was received.

### Right to Move

- During the **30 days** after the Notice was served, the relocating person may not move to a different school district with the children unless they have a court order allowing the move.
- After the 30 days, if **no Objection is filed**, the relocating person may move with the children without getting a court order allowing the move.
- After the 30 days, if **an Objection has been filed**, the relocating person may move with the children pending the final hearing on the Objection **unless**:
  - The other party gets a court order saying the children cannot move, **OR**
  - The other party has scheduled a hearing to take place no more than 15 days after the date the Objection was served on the relocating person.
  - (However, the relocating person may ask the court for an order allowing the move even though a hearing is pending if the relocating person believes that they or a child is at unreasonable risk of harm.)

The court may make a different decision about the move at a final hearing on the Objection.

### Parenting Plan After Move

If the relocating person served a proposed Parenting Plan with the Notice, and if no Objection is filed within 30 days after the Notice was served (or if the parties agree):

- Both parties may follow that proposed plan without being held in contempt of the Parenting Plan that was in place before the move.
- However, the proposed plan cannot be enforced by contempt unless it has been approved by a court.
- Either party may ask the court to approve the proposed plan. Use form **Ex Parte Motion for Final Order Changing Parenting Plan – No Objection to Moving with Children (FL Relocate 706)**.

### Forms

You can find forms about moving with children at:
- The Washington State Courts' website: www.courts.wa.gov/forms
- Washington LawHelp: www.washingtonlawhelp.org
- The Superior Court Clerk's office or county law library (for a fee)

> *This is a summary of the law. The complete law is in RCW 26.09.430 through 26.09.480.*

## 14. Other Provisions

### a. Address/Telephone Numbers

- Both parents shall be required to keep the other advised of their **current residential and employment addresses**, as well as their telephone numbers at all times.
- Both parents shall notify the other regarding any **permanent changes to their work schedule**.
- Neither parent shall telephone the other or address mail to the other at their place of employment except to discuss an **emergency situation**.
- Each parent will keep the other parent informed at all times of an **emergency contact** if that parent and/or the children will be out-of-town.

### b. Telephone Access

When the children are not residing with a given parent, they shall be permitted **reasonable telephone/video call and email access** with that parent at reasonable times and for reasonable durations.

### c. Mail Access

The parents and the children shall enjoy **unrestricted mail and email access** to each other.

### d. Access to Records

- Each parent shall have the right and responsibility to ensure that the children attend school and other scheduled activities while in that parent's care.
- Each parent shall have **full and equal access** to the education, daycare and health records of the children (except to the extent that a separate consent may need to be obtained for a child as provided by law).
- Both parents shall have equal and independent authority, as provided by statute, to **confer with the school**.

### e. Medical Directive

- Both parents shall follow the **medical directives** of the children's health care providers and physicians including giving prescribed medication.
- Each parent shall notify the other parent promptly of any medication that has been prescribed for the children.

### f. Attending Activities

- The parents shall be responsible for keeping themselves advised of **athletic, academic and social events** in which the children participate.
- **Both parents may participate** in school and extracurricular activities for the children regardless of whether the activity falls during their residential time.
- Both parents shall be responsible for **transporting the children to all agreed upon activities** during their residential time.
  - **PROVIDED THAT** each parent may elect to **skip up to one weekend activity(ies) a quarter** to attend other planned activities or trips with the children.

### g. Children's Involvement

- Neither parent shall ask the children to make decisions or requests involving the residential schedule.
- Neither parent shall encourage the children to change their primary residence or encourage the children to believe it is their choice to do so.
- Neither parent shall discuss changes to the residential schedule which have not been agreed to by both parents in advance.
- Neither parent shall advise the children of the status of child support payments or other legal matters regarding the parent's relationship.
- Neither parent shall use the children, directly or indirectly, to gather information about the other parent or to take verbal messages to the other parent.

### h. Homework

- Each parent shall ensure the children complete **all homework** they are required to complete during that parent's residential time.
- Each parent is responsible for keeping themselves apprised of the children's homework assignments.

### i. Derogatory Comments

- Neither parent shall make **derogatory comments** about the other parent or allow anyone else to do so in the children's presence.
- Neither parent shall allow or encourage the children to make derogatory comments about the other parent.

### j. Travel Notification

Each parent shall inform the other parent when that parent plans to be away from their residence with the children overnight. The information to be provided shall include:
- Duration of the period
- The destination(s)
- Destination telephone number(s) including cell phone numbers

### k. International Travel

- Neither parent may leave the **United States (excluding Canada)** with the children without written notice to the other parent and agreement.
  - E-mail or OFW notice shall suffice
  - Agreement shall **not be unreasonably withheld**
- Any disputes about whether a parent may travel internationally with the children shall be submitted to **dispute resolution**.
- If an emergency, the party may seek an **ex parte order** with notice to the other parent.
- Travel with the children to any country that is **not a signatory to The Hague Convention** on the Civil Aspects of International Child Abduction with a US treaty is **prohibited** unless the non-traveling parent signs a **written consent** (authorization by e-mail is insufficient) containing the detailed itinerary of the trip and agreeing that the children will be returned on a specified date.

#### Passport and Travel Documentation:

- If necessary for travel, the parents shall cooperate in obtaining the children's passports and keeping the passports valid.
- **Yi Chen shall hold the children's passports.**
- If visas or other documentation are required for foreign travel, the parents shall cooperate in good faith to obtain such visas and provide such documentation.
- The **United States is the habitual residence** of the children and a refusal to return the children to the United States by either parent shall be conclusively deemed **wrongful under the Convention**.

#### Special Provision for Travel to China:

When the father travels to China, he shall return to the United States so that the children have **2 days (48 hours) to reacclimate to the pacific time zone** before being returned to the mother and commencing school.

### l. Routine of Children

- Both parents shall try to maintain the children's **regular routine and bedtime schedule** during their residential time with them.
- Both parents shall facilitate this by providing timely information and updates about the children's schedule and routines.

### m. Emergency Notification

Each parent shall notify the other **promptly but in any event no later than 24 hours** of receipt of extraordinary information regarding the children, such as:
- Emergency medical care
- Major school discipline
- Unusual or unexplained absence from the home
- Contact with police or other legal authority

### n. Agreements and Waiver

- The parents are free at any time to make **agreements between the two of them** that are different than the residential and nonresidential provisions of this parenting plan.
- **Agreements shall be in writing** (agreements documented by email are sufficient).
- An agreement to act differently concerning a provision on one or more occasions **does not result in a waiver** of that provision in the future, nor does it result in a waiver of any other provisions or create adequate cause for future modification.

### o. Parental Communication

The parents shall communicate via **Our Family Wizard**, except in the case of an emergency in which case text or phone calls may be used.

### p. Corporal Punishment

- **No corporal punishment** may be used with the children.
- The children may not be locked in a room as a form of punishment.

### q. Therapy

The parents shall each **continue in individual therapy** and they must provide copies of both the **Parental Evaluation completed by Dr. English**, and this **Final Parenting Plan** to their providers.
