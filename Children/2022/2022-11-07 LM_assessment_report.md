### Summary of Findings
Based on Laurence’s performance on the WPPSI-IV, his estimated intellectual level falls in the Superior range, with a Full Scale IQ (FSIQ) of 128, which places him at the 97th percentile rank of same-aged peers. Similarly, Laurence’s General Ability Index (GAI) was 127, in the Superior range and at the 96th percentile rank of same-aged peers.

#### Verbal Comprehension Index (VCI)
Measures knowledge acquired from a child’s environment, verbal concept formation, and verbal reasoning. Laurence’s score of 123 (94th percentile) is classified as Superior, suggesting advanced receptive and expressive language skills, general knowledge, and verbal conceptual reasoning.

#### Visual Spatial Index (VSI)
Measures visual-spatial processing, understanding of part-whole relationships, attentiveness to visual detail, nonverbal concept formation, and visual-motor integration. Laurence’s score of 141 (99.7th percentile) falls in the Very Superior range, making this an area of relative strength. The Block Design subtest was a particular strength.

#### Fluid Reasoning Index (FRI)
Measures concept formation, inductive reasoning, and conceptual thinking. Laurence’s score of 111 (77th percentile) is classified as High Average, making this an area of relative weakness compared to his other scores. The Matrix Reasoning subtest was a relative weakness.

#### Working Memory Index (WMI)
Measures visual working memory and the ability to resist proactive interference. Laurence’s score of 113 (81st percentile) is classified in the High Average range. The Zoo Locations subtest was a relative weakness.

#### Processing Speed Index (PSI)
Measures cognitive processing speed and the ability to quickly scan and understand simple visual information. Laurence’s score of 115 (84th percentile) is classified in the High Average range.

## Summary and Recommendations
Cognitive testing indicates that Laurence’s Full Scale IQ is greater than 97% of same-aged peers. His visuospatial abilities are beyond almost all peers. Verbal awareness and reasoning are quite advanced, and his nonverbal reasoning, working memory, and processing speed abilities are strong as well. Laurence demonstrates a number of cognitive assets on which to build.

Laurence displayed Superior cognitive abilities with a relative strength in visual spatial processing and a relative weakness in fluid reasoning. Laurence’s FSIQ and GAI fall in the Superior range compared to same-aged peers. He was cooperative and friendly, demonstrating good effort and following instructions throughout testing. He is likely to do well in a program that challenges him cognitively and academically. It is recommended that Laurence be considered for advanced learning opportunities.
