## 🧠 Social Emotional Behavioral Risk Screener (SAEBRS/mySAEBRS)
[cite_start]SAEBRS is a survey that classroom teachers complete for all students 3 times a year [cite: 178][cite_start]. mySAEBRS is a survey where students in grades 2-5 self-reflect on their behaviors and opinions about school[cite: 179]. [cite_start]SAEBRS is **not** a diagnostic tool[cite: 182].

### **Areas Surveyed**
* **Social**: Can the student wait their turn? Is the student respectful? [cite_start]Does the student get along with peers? [cite: 184]
* **Academic**: Does the student pay attention in class? Does the student participate in class? Can the student work independently? [cite_start]Does the student like school? [cite: 185, 186]
* **Emotional-behavioral**: Does the student feel sad, nervous, and/ or happy? [cite_start]Does the student try new things? [cite: 187]

### **Levels of Support Identified**

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Low Need** | [cite_start]Your child does not need regular additional support[cite: 189]. | (All students will receive ongoing social-emotional skill building through Second Step) [cite_start][cite: 190]. | |
| **Some Need** | [cite_start]Your child might need some additional support in this area[cite: 194]. | (Ongoing social-emotional skill building through Second Step will support skill development) [cite_start][cite: 195]. | |
| **High Need** | [cite_start]Your child may need regular additional support in this area[cite: 196]. | (Ongoing social-emotional skill building through Second Step will support skill development) [cite_start][cite: 197]. | |

## 🏡 Supporting Learning at Home
[cite_start]If you have any questions about the scores, please connect with your child's classroom teacher[cite: 154, 217].

### **A. Independent Reading**
[cite_start]Reading independently is one of the best ways to promote literacy[cite: 221].
* [cite_start]Your school or local librarian can assist in finding high interest, age-appropriate books[cite: 222]. [cite_start]**Bring the Lexile level (595)** from this report to your librarian to help[cite: 223].
* [cite_start]If your student has difficulty reading grade-level text, you may want to consider using **Learning Ally**[cite: 224].
    * [cite_start]Learning Ally is a digital tool that combines audio books with visual text and cueing, so students can read along to grade level, high interest text[cite: 225].
    * [cite_start]Any student who has difficulty reading grade level text has access through Clever or can request access[cite: 226].

### **B. Using i-Ready at Home (MyPath)**
[cite_start]i-Ready is available for students to use at home for personalized learning and practice in Math or Reading[cite: 229].
* [cite_start]i-Ready has created a learning plan specifically for your student in a program called **MyPath**[cite: 230]. [cite_start]MyPath provides lessons that are assigned to students based on how they answered questions on the i-Ready assessments[cite: 231].
* [cite_start]If your student isn't already completing **30-50 minutes per week** of MyPath lessons at school, you may want to have your student complete MyPath lessons at home[cite: 233].
* [cite_start]Students should complete MyPath lessons **independently**[cite: 235]. [cite_start]If a student doesn't pass the lesson the first time, *then* they should ask an adult for help on the learning portion of the lesson, and complete the practice independently[cite: 235].

#### **Directions for Accessing i-Ready**
1.  [cite_start]Access i-Ready through **Clever**[cite: 238].
    > [cite_start]**IMPORTANT for iPad users**: if using an iPad at home, download both the Clever app and the i-Ready app THEN access i-Ready through the Clever app[cite: 239].
2.  [cite_start]Review your student's **i-Ready Dashboard** with your student to see their progress and what they are working on[cite: 244].
    * [cite_start]Directions for accessing the dashboard are available in the following languages: Arabic, Bengali, Cambodian (Khmer), Chinese (Mandarin), Haitian Creole, Hmong, Korean, Portuguese, Russian, Samoan, Somali, Spanish, Tagalog, Yupik[cite: 247, 248].
