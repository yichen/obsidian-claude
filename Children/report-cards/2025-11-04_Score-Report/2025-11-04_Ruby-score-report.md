## 🧠 Social Emotional Behavioral Risk Screener (SAEBRS/mySAEBRS)
[cite_start]SAEBRS (Social Academic Emotional Behavioral Risk Screener) is a survey that classroom teachers complete for all students 3 times a year [cite: 53][cite_start]. mySAEBRS is a survey where students in grades 2-5 self-reflect on their behaviors and opinions about school[cite: 54]. [cite_start]These two surveys provide information about how the student may think and feel at one point in time[cite: 55]. [cite_start]It provides parents and teachers with information about what the student may need to feel successful in the school and classroom environment[cite: 56]. [cite_start]SAEBRS is **not** a diagnostic tool[cite: 57].

### **Areas Surveyed**
* **Social**: Can the student wait their turn? Is the student respectful? [cite_start]Does the student get along with peers? [cite: 59]
* **Academic**: Does the student pay attention in class? Does the student participate in class? Can the student work independently? [cite_start]Does the student like school? [cite: 60, 61]
* **Emotional-behavioral**: Does the student feel sad, nervous, and/ or happy? [cite_start]Does the student try new things? [cite: 62]

### **Levels of Support Identified**
| Level | Description | Note | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Low Need** | [cite_start]Your child does not need regular additional support[cite: 64]. | (All students will receive ongoing social-emotional skill building through Second Step) [cite_start][cite: 65]. | |
| **Some Need** | [cite_start]Your child might need some additional support in this area[cite: 69]. | (Ongoing social-emotional skill building through Second Step will support skill development) [cite_start][cite: 70]. | |
| **High Need** | [cite_start]Your child may need regular additional support in this area[cite: 71]. | (Ongoing social-emotional skill building through Second Step will support skill development) [cite_start][cite: 72]. | |
| **NS** | **No Score** | [cite_start]The student may not have taken the assessment, or the data may not have been received within the testing window[cite: 77]. | |

> [cite_start]A staff member may contact you to discuss your child if results show: High risk overall [cite: 73] [cite_start]or High risk in emotional area[cite: 74]. [cite_start]However, if school staff have been in ongoing communication about your child's social, emotional and/or behavioral needs, you may not receive this additional communication[cite: 75, 76].

## 🏡 Supporting Learning at Home
[cite_start]If you have any questions about the scores, please connect with your child's classroom teacher[cite: 29, 92].

### **A. Independent Reading**
[cite_start]Reading independently is one of the best ways to promote literacy[cite: 96].
* [cite_start]Your school or local librarian can assist in finding high interest, age-appropriate books[cite: 97]. [cite_start]Bring the Lexile level (440) from page 1 of this report to your librarian to help[cite: 98, 41].
* [cite_start]If your student has difficulty reading grade-level text, you may want to consider using **Learning Ally**[cite: 99].
    * [cite_start]Learning Ally is a digital tool that combines audio books with visual text and cueing, so students can read along to grade level, high interest text[cite: 100].
    * [cite_start]Any student who has difficulty reading grade level text has access through Clever or can request access[cite: 101].

### **B. Using i-Ready at Home (MyPath)**
[cite_start]i-Ready is available for students to use at home for personalized learning and practice in Math or Reading[cite: 104].
* [cite_start]i-Ready has created a learning plan specifically for your student in a program called **MyPath**[cite: 105]. [cite_start]MyPath provides lessons that are assigned to students based on how they answered questions on the i-Ready assessments[cite: 106].
* [cite_start]If your student isn't already completing **30-50 minutes per week** of MyPath lessons at school, you may want to have your student complete MyPath lessons at home[cite: 108].
* [cite_start]Students should complete MyPath lessons **independently**[cite: 110]. [cite_start]If a student doesn't pass the lesson the first time, *then* they should ask an adult for help on the learning portion of the lesson, and complete the practice independently[cite: 110].
