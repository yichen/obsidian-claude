[Glue Work](https://noidea.dog/glue)

What are the glue work?
- onboarding the junior engineers
- updating the roadmap
- talking to the users
- noticing the things that got dropped
- asking questions on design documents
- making sure everybody is going in the same direction.

When is *glue work* not good?
- It can be career limiting if you do it too early in career.

Concrete Examples of Glue Work
* Answering questions for team (on support channels). The team stops getting too many interruptions.
* Talking with a nearby team, and found out they seem to have a different idea of what problems they're all solving and they're going a different direction. Set up a meeting to re-align the direction.
* Set up a mentorship program and writes onboarding documents to help new hires.
* Attributed outages to lack of tests. Gathers a bunch of senior people and keeps pushing until they agree on coding standards for the whole org.
* Discovers a good engnineer needs information from another team. Talked to people in real life, figures out the confusion, and unblocks the engineer.


Advice
- I advise people to choose deliberately. Choose a role that you'll feel successful and happy and proud to say you do, and that will teach you skills you want. Do a job you’re excited by. You will learn to get good at it by doing it. I feel like we don't admit it often enough enough that most of the time, we won't do a job well on day one. The vast majority of our learning happens on the job.
- Tell the story. You needs artifacts of your work that show your impact and tell the story. Your manager should be able to tell the same story. You should be creating and saving artifats that back up this narrative: design proposals, meeting notes, group emails, crucial points where you made thing happen.
- If you are not getting promoted (valued) for glue work, stop doing glue work. Do exactly the thing on the job ladder, even if it means letting more important things drop. Eg: easily quantifiable technical work, write a bunch of code, something that is unarguably "technical", declare a lot of things not your problem. Stop interviewing, stop organising the off-sites, stop onboarding, stop fielding requests from users, stop anything that sounds like team building. Do not curate the team roadmap. Crucially: don't catch things that are about to drop. 
- If you only do glue, you will only get better at glue, you are hurting your future self. Make it clear that it's OK -- and normal -- to lear at work during work hours.
