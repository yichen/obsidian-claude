* We did a mighty shed from Pan Abode. Their main line of business is full scale custom homes but they started doing under 200 sq foot structures as the tiny home craze became a thing. If you stay under certain sq foot and height requirements you don’t need a permit. But you have to also be wary of lot line setbacks and not build a structure in the setback. That’s where a conversation with the City is probably helpful just to make sure you’re not going to end up in trouble.
* I’m planning on adding one from studio shed. I like the Portland model. A couple of my patients have used them and loved it! [link](https://www.studio-shed-selection.com/products/signature-series/portland/?fbclid=IwAR0zLb8llCoDlWasj7fEoj6KmSueLIOVdTukuckboLZa2PgkMK8mC851H7c)
* You don’t need a permit if under 200sqft but if you live with an HOA, they may have restrictions. We used Better Built Barns but we had to finish the inside ourselves.
